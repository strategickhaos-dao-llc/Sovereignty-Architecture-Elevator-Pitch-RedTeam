### Crisis Classification System

#### Severity Levels
```markdown
## Crisis Level Definitions

### Level 1: Minor Incident
**Definition**: Limited impact, manageable within normal operations
**Examples**: Small payment delay, minor technical issue, isolated complaint
**Response**: Department head manages, notification to leadership
**Escalation Criteria**: Not resolved within 48 hours or impact increases

### Level 2: Moderate Crisis
**Definition**: Significant impact on operations, requires management attention
**Examples**: Service provider outage, employment dispute, negative media story
**Response**: Crisis response team activated, daily updates to board
**Escalation Criteria**: Threat to mission delivery or financial stability

### Level 3: Major Crisis
**Definition**: Substantial threat to organization viability
**Examples**: Royalty payment stoppage, major lawsuit, regulatory investigation
**Response**: Full crisis team, emergency board meeting, external advisors engaged
**Escalation Criteria**: Existential threat to organization

### Level 4: Existential Crisis
**Definition**: Immediate threat to organization survival
**Examples**: Loss of nonprofit status, catastrophic financial loss, criminal charges
**Response**: Emergency board convening, possible suspension of operations, asset protection
```

### Crisis Response Procedures

#### Initial Response Checklist (First 24 Hours)
```markdown
- [ ] **Assess Situation**
  - [ ] Gather facts (avoid assumptions)
  - [ ] Classify severity level
  - [ ] Identify immediate threats
  - [ ] Determine crisis type

- [ ] **Activate Response**
  - [ ] Alert crisis response team
  - [ ] Convene emergency meeting (virtual or in-person)
  - [ ] Assign roles and responsibilities
  - [ ] Establish communication protocols

- [ ] **Contain Crisis**
  - [ ] Take immediate actions to limit damage
  - [ ] Secure critical assets and information
  - [ ] Implement relevant contingency plan
  - [ ] Preserve evidence and documentation

- [ ] **Communicate**
  - [ ] Internal notification (staff, board, volunteers)
  - [ ] External communication (if needed)
  - [ ] Stakeholder updates (donors, partners, regulators)
  - [ ] Media response (if applicable)

- [ ] **Document**
  - [ ] Begin detailed incident log
  - [ ] Preserve all relevant documents and communications
  - [ ] Photograph/document physical evidence
  - [ ] Track all decisions and actions
```

## Financial Crisis Response

### Royalty Payment Interruption

#### CONTINGENCY PLAN F-001: NinjaTrader Payment Stops

**Scenario**: NinjaTrader or primary royalty licensor stops making payments

**Trigger Events**:
- Payment more than 15 days late
- Notification of payment reduction or termination
- Licensee bankruptcy filing
- Contact from licensee about payment issues

**Immediate Actions (Days 1-3)**:
```markdown
## Phase 1: Assessment and Communication

### Hour 1-4
- [ ] Verify payment was actually due (check agreement)
- [ ] Check bank account for possible deposit delay
- [ ] Review communication for any prior notice
- [ ] Calculate immediate cash flow impact
- [ ] Alert Crisis Response Team

### Day 1
- [ ] Contact licensee's designated contact person
- [ ] Send formal inquiry via email (documented)
- [ ] Check licensee's public financial status
- [ ] Notify board president and treasurer
- [ ] Review royalty agreement terms

### Days 2-3
- [ ] Send formal payment demand letter (legal counsel)
- [ ] Assess reserve fund adequacy
- [ ] Identify expense reduction options
- [ ] Schedule emergency board meeting
- [ ] Prepare stakeholder communication plan
```

**Short-term Actions (Week 1)**:
```yaml
week_1_actions:
  legal:
    - "Engage attorney to review rights and remedies"
    - "Send formal notice of default"
    - "Assess litigation vs. negotiation options"
    - "Review insurance coverage (if applicable)"
    
  financial:
    - "Activate reserve fund protocol"
    - "Implement immediate expense freeze"
    - "Prioritize critical expenses only"
    - "Defer non-essential payments"
    - "Model cash flow scenarios (30, 60, 90 days)"
    
  operational:
    - "Brief leadership team on situation"
    - "Prepare staff for potential impacts"
    - "Identify which programs can continue"
    - "Defer any planned initiatives"
    
  communications:
    - "Prepare internal communication"
    - "Draft donor/funder communication (hold until needed)"
    - "Monitor for any public awareness"
```

**Medium-term Actions (Weeks 2-4)**:
```markdown
## Phase 2: Stabilization and Resolution

### Financial Management
- [ ] Weekly cash flow monitoring
- [ ] Expense reduction implementation (target: 30% cut)
- [ ] Revenue diversification acceleration
- [ ] Emergency fundraising if needed
- [ ] Negotiate payment plans with vendors

### Legal Strategy
- [ ] Pursue legal remedies per counsel advice
- [ ] Negotiate settlement or payment plan with licensee
- [ ] Exercise audit rights if warranted
- [ ] Consider security interest enforcement

### Organizational Adjustment
- [ ] Implement revised operating plan
- [ ] Staff reductions if unavoidable (last resort)
- [ ] Program prioritization and scaling
- [ ] Board engagement in fundraising
- [ ] Stakeholder communication as appropriate
```

**Long-term Recovery (Months 2-6)**:
```yaml
recovery_phase:
  strategic_goals:
    - "Achieve financial sustainability without royalty income"
    - "Diversify revenue to prevent future dependency"
    - "Resolve legal/business issues with licensee"
    - "Restore organizational confidence"
    
  key_actions:
    revenue:
      - "Launch major donor campaign"
      - "Pursue grant opportunities"
      - "Develop alternative earned income"
      - "Seek additional royalty arrangements"
      
    operations:
      - "Right-size organization to available funding"
      - "Focus on highest-impact programs"
      - "Rebuild reserves gradually"
      
    governance:
      - "Update policies to prevent recurrence"
      - "Strengthen financial oversight"
      - "Improve risk management"
```

## Legal Crisis Response

### Lawsuit or Legal Threat

#### CONTINGENCY PLAN L-001: Significant Lawsuit Filed

**Scenario**: Organization is sued or receives credible threat of lawsuit

**Immediate Actions (Day 1)**:
```markdown
## Legal Crisis Response Protocol

### Upon Notice of Lawsuit
- [ ] Notify insurance carrier immediately (within 24 hours)
- [ ] Engage legal counsel (retained attorney or refer to D&O insurance)
- [ ] Preserve all relevant documents (litigation hold)
- [ ] Alert crisis response team and board president
- [ ] Assess immediate operational impacts

### Document Preservation
- [ ] Issue litigation hold notice to all staff
- [ ] Identify custodians of relevant documents
- [ ] Suspend routine document destruction
- [ ] Preserve electronic communications
- [ ] Secure physical documents
```

**Crisis Management Structure**:
```yaml
lawsuit_management:
  legal_team:
    lead_counsel: "Retained or appointed by insurer"
    in_house: "General counsel or board member"
    specialists: "As needed (IP, employment, etc.)"
    
  board_role:
    involvement: "Oversight and strategic decisions"
    committee: "Ad hoc litigation committee if needed"
    updates: "Weekly during active phase"
    
  communication_strategy:
    internal: "Need-to-know basis, avoid speculation"
    external: "Coordinate all statements with legal counsel"
    media: "Prepared statement only, if needed"
    stakeholders: "As appropriate, legally reviewed"
```

**Response Timeline**:
```markdown
## Litigation Response Schedule

### Week 1
- [ ] Legal team assembled and briefed
- [ ] Document preservation implemented
- [ ] Insurance claim filed
- [ ] Board briefed
- [ ] Response deadline calculated
- [ ] Initial defense strategy developed

### Weeks 2-4
- [ ] Legal response filed (answer, motion to dismiss, etc.)
- [ ] Discovery plan developed
- [ ] Settlement assessment
- [ ] Regular board updates
- [ ] Budget impact assessed

### Ongoing
- [ ] Monthly litigation status reports to board
- [ ] Quarterly strategic review
- [ ] Settlement discussions if appropriate
- [ ] Preparation for trial if necessary
```

### Regulatory Investigation

#### CONTINGENCY PLAN L-002: Government Investigation

**Scenario**: IRS audit, state AG investigation, or other regulatory inquiry

**Immediate Actions**:
```yaml
regulatory_investigation_response:
  hour_1:
    - "Notify board president and treasurer"
    - "Engage specialized legal counsel"
    - "Preserve all relevant documents"
    - "Do not destroy any records"
    - "Prepare for initial response"
    
  day_1:
    - "Full board notification"
    - "Assemble response team"
    - "Review scope of investigation"
    - "Assess cooperation strategy"
    - "Identify required documents"
    
  week_1:
    - "Develop production plan"
    - "Assign internal coordinators"
    - "Establish communication protocols"
    - "Begin document review process"
    - "Prepare initial response or production"
```

**Investigation Management**:
```markdown
## Managing Regulatory Inquiry

### Key Principles
- **Cooperate Fully**: Provide requested information timely and completely
- **Legal Guidance**: All responses reviewed by counsel
- **Document Everything**: Keep detailed records of all interactions
- **Truthfulness**: Never provide false or misleading information
- **Professional**: Maintain respectful relationship with investigators

### Red Flags to Avoid
- ❌ Document destruction after investigation begins
- ❌ Incomplete or selective responses
- ❌ Lack of legal counsel involvement
- ❌ Uncoordinated responses from multiple people
- ❌ Defensive or adversarial posture
```

```yaml
    - "Review succession plan"
    
  day_1:
    - "Emergency board meeting (virtual acceptable)"
    - "Appoint interim executive director"
    - "Notify key stakeholders"
    - "Ensure operational continuity"
    - "Begin transition planning"
```

**Interim Leadership Plan**:
```markdown
## Interim Executive Director Protocol

### Immediate Appointment (Day 1)
**First Choice**: Chief Operating Officer or Senior Director  
**Second Choice**: Board member with operational experience  
**Third Choice**: External interim executive (consultant)

### Interim Authority
- Full operational authority
- Budget authority per policy
- Personnel decisions (with board approval for major actions)
- Board liaison responsibilities
- Limited strategic changes without board approval

### Transition Period
- 30-day assessment period
- 90-day stabilization period
- 6-month maximum interim assignment
- Permanent search begins immediately
```

## Reputational Crisis Response

### Negative Media or Publicity

#### CONTINGENCY PLAN R-001: Reputational Attack

**Scenario**: Significant negative publicity, media coverage, or social media campaign

**Crisis Communications Protocol**:
```yaml
communications_response:
  severity_assessment:
    minor:
      definition: "Limited reach, low credibility"
      response: "Monitor, respond if appropriate"
      
    moderate:
      definition: "Regional reach, some credibility"
      response: "Prepare statement, proactive outreach"
      
    major:
      definition: "National reach, high credibility"
      response: "Full crisis response, media engagement"
      
    critical:
      definition: "Viral spread, mainstream media"
      response: "Crisis team, external PR firm, board involvement"
```

**Response Timeline**:
```markdown
## Hour-by-Hour Response Plan

### Hour 1: Assessment
- [ ] Identify source and content of attack/story
- [ ] Determine accuracy of allegations
- [ ] Assess potential spread and impact
- [ ] Alert crisis communications team
- [ ] Begin monitoring social media and news

### Hours 2-4: Strategy
- [ ] Develop key messages
- [ ] Determine response approach (ignore, correct, engage)
- [ ] Prepare statement if needed
- [ ] Identify spokesperson
- [ ] Brief board and leadership

### Hours 4-12: Response
- [ ] Issue statement if appropriate
- [ ] Contact key stakeholders directly
- [ ] Respond to media inquiries
- [ ] Post factual information
- [ ] Monitor and adjust strategy

### Hours 12-24: Stabilization
- [ ] Continue monitoring
- [ ] Follow up with stakeholders
- [ ] Assess effectiveness of response
- [ ] Plan ongoing communication
```

**Communication Principles**:
```markdown
## Crisis Communication Guidelines

### Do's
✅ Respond quickly but thoughtfully
✅ Stick to facts
✅ Be transparent about what you know and don't know
✅ Show empathy and concern
✅ Demonstrate accountability
✅ Provide regular updates
✅ Coordinate all responses

### Don'ts
❌ Speculate or guess
❌ Say "no comment"
❌ Blame others
❌ Make promises you can't keep
❌ Respond emotionally
❌ Ignore legitimate concerns
❌ Allow multiple uncoordinated spokespersons
```

## Cybersecurity Incident Response

### Data Breach or Cyber Attack

#### CONTINGENCY PLAN C-001: Cybersecurity Incident

**Scenario**: Ransomware, data breach, or other cyber attack

**Immediate Response (First Hour)**:
```yaml
cyber_incident_response:
  detection:
    - "Verify incident is occurring"
    - "Identify affected systems"
    - "Assess scope of compromise"
    
  containment:
    - "Isolate affected systems from network"
    - "Preserve evidence (do not power off without forensic guidance)"
    - "Change credentials for potentially compromised accounts"
    - "Block malicious IP addresses or domains"
    
  notification:
    - "Alert IT team or managed security provider"
    - "Notify crisis response team"
    - "Contact cyber insurance carrier"
    - "Engage cybersecurity forensic firm if needed"
```

**Incident Management Team**:
```yaml
cyber_response_team:
  incident_commander:
    role: "Overall coordination"
    person: "CTO or Executive Director"
    
  technical_lead:
    role: "Technical response and forensics"
    person: "IT Director or external firm"
    
  legal_counsel:
    role: "Legal obligations and privilege"
    person: "General counsel or retained attorney"
    
  communications:
    role: "Internal and external communication"
    person: "Communications Director"
    
  business_continuity:
    role: "Maintain operations during response"
    person: "Operations Manager"
```

**Response Phases**:
```markdown
## Cybersecurity Incident Response Phases

### Phase 1: Identification and Containment (Hours 1-12)
- Verify and scope the incident
- Isolate affected systems
- Preserve evidence
- Engage forensic experts
- Activate incident response team

### Phase 2: Investigation (Days 1-7)
- Forensic analysis of affected systems
- Determine attack vector and timeline
- Identify data accessed or exfiltrated
- Assess overall impact
- Document findings

### Phase 3: Notification (Days 3-14, as legally required)
- Legal analysis of notification requirements
- Notify affected individuals if PII compromised
- Regulatory notifications (e.g., state AG, HHS)
- Law enforcement notification
- Insurance claim filing

### Phase 4: Recovery (Days 7-30)
- Remove malware or attacker access
- Restore systems from clean backups
- Implement enhanced security controls
- Monitor for re-infection
- Return to normal operations

### Phase 5: Post-Incident (Days 30-90)
- Comprehensive incident review
- Update incident response plan
- Implement lessons learned
- Enhanced security measures
- Training for personnel
```

**Ransomware-Specific Protocol**:
```yaml
ransomware_response:
  do_not_pay_ransom_without:
    - "Legal counsel advice"
    - "Law enforcement notification"
    - "Insurance carrier consultation"
    - "Board approval"
    - "Assessment of alternatives"
    
  ransom_decision_factors:
    consider_paying_if:
      - "No viable backup exists"
      - "Data is critical and irreplaceable"
      - "Cost is reasonable relative to alternatives"
      - "Legal and ethical implications acceptable"
      
    do_not_pay_if:
      - "Clean backups exist"
      - "Payment would be illegal"
      - "Funding terrorism concerns"
      - "No guarantee of decryption"
```
