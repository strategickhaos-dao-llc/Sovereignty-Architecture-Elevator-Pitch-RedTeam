## Core Neural Components

Scientists view the nervous system as a complex network for information processing, with neurons as the basic units that transmit electrochemical signals. As of 2025, advances in connectomics (e.g., full brain maps from projects like the BRAIN Initiative) and optogenetics reveal dynamic plasticity, where synapses strengthen or weaken based on activity, enabling learning. Neural biology integrates molecular, cellular, and systems levels, with AI models simulating emergent behaviors like consciousness.

## Neural Signaling & Plasticity

Neural signaling involves action potentials (all-or-none electrical spikes) and synaptic transmission. Plasticity (e.g., LTP/LTD) allows rewiring, with 2025 insights from single-cell RNA sequencing showing how gene expression changes underpin learning. Disorders like Alzheimer's highlight synaptic dysfunction.

| Neural Process | Scientific Description | Biological Analog | Cellular Component | Chemical/Element | Mapping Quality |
|----------------|------------------------|-------------------|--------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Action Potential** | Rapid depolarization-repolarization cycle | Electrical pulse | Voltage-gated channels | Chloride (Cl) - inhibitory currents | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very Strong - Signal propagation |
| **Synaptic Transmission** | Neurotransmitter release into cleft, binding receptors | Chemical messenger | Vesicles and receptors | Glutamate (complex) - excitatory neurotransmitter | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very Strong - Information transfer |
| **Long-Term Potentiation (LTP)** | Strengthening of synapses via repeated stimulation | Memory engraving | NMDA receptor activation | Copper (Cu) - in synaptic proteins | ⭐⭐⭐ Strong - Learning mechanism |
| **Long-Term Depression (LTD)** | Weakening of synapses | Forgetting/pruning | AMPA receptor removal | Manganese (Mn) - enzymatic cofactor | ⭐⭐⭐ Strong - Circuit refinement |
| **Neurogenesis** | Birth of new neurons from stem cells | System upgrade | Hippocampal dentate gyrus | Boron (B) - growth factor support | ⭐⭐ Medium - Adult brain plasticity |
| **Axonal Transport** | Movement of organelles along microtubules | Internal logistics | Kinesin/dynein motors | Sulfur (S) - in cysteine bonds | ⭐⭐⭐ Strong - Material distribution |
| **Gap Junctions** | Direct electrical connections | Shortcut links | Connexin proteins | Hydrogen (H) - in bonding | ⭐⭐ Medium - Synchronized activity |

## Key Insights from This Mapping

### Strong Analogies (Actually Useful)

1. **Neuron = Processing Unit** - Like a CPU with inputs/outputs.
2. **Synapse = API Endpoint** - Modular connections for data transfer.
3. **Plasticity = Machine Learning** - Adjusts weights based on experience.
4. **Action Potential = Binary Signal** - All-or-none for reliable transmission.
5. **Glia = Infrastructure Support** - Essential but often overlooked.

### Weak Analogies (Poetic Only)

1. Elements like Boron/Aluminum (minor or indirect roles).
2. Specific disorders as "models" (too disease-focused).
3. Slime mold direct parallels (neural is more hierarchical).

### Emergent Properties Worth Noting

1. **Complexity from Simplicity** - Billions of neurons create mind.
2. **Resilience** - Redundancy and plasticity for robustness.
3. **Hierarchy with Feedback** - Bottom-up and top-down control.
4. **Integration** - Senses, emotions, cognition intertwined.
