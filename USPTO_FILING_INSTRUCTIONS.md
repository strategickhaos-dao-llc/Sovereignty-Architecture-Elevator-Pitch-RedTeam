### Step 5: Fee Information

1. In the **"Fees"** section, select **"Micro Entity"** status
2. To qualify as a micro entity, you must certify that:
   - [ ] You qualify as a small entity
   - [ ] Neither you nor the inventor has been named on more than 4 previously filed patent applications
   - [ ] Neither you nor the inventor had gross income exceeding $200,000 in the previous calendar year
   - [ ] You have not assigned or obligated to assign rights to an entity that had gross income exceeding $200,000
3. Check the certification box
4. The fee will automatically calculate to **$75**

**If you don't qualify for micro entity status:**
- Small entity fee: $150
- Standard entity fee: $300

### Common Questions

**Q: What if I make a mistake in the filing?**  
A: You can file a corrected version or supplemental information. The original filing date is preserved.

**Q: Can I file this for someone else?**  
A: Yes, but they must be listed as the inventor. You can file on their behalf as the applicant.

**Q: What if someone already filed a similar patent?**  
A: Your filing still creates prior art. If they filed after you, your filing defeats their patent. If they filed before you, both exist as prior art.

**Q: Is this legal advice?**  
A: No. This is educational information about the USPTO filing process. Consult a patent attorney for specific legal advice.

**Q: Will this actually stop patent trolls?**  
A: Yes. A USPTO provisional filing is one of the strongest forms of defensive prior art. Patent examiners must cite it, and it creates a high bar for anyone trying to patent these concepts.
