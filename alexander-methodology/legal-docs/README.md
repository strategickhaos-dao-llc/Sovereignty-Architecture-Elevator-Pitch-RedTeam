## Why 501(c)(3)?

### Benefits

1. **Tax-Deductible Donations**
   - Individual donors can deduct contributions
   - Corporate giving programs applicable
   - Foundation grants accessible

2. **Grant Eligibility**
   - Federal research grants (NSF, NIH, etc.)
   - Private foundation funding
   - International research collaborations

3. **Tax Exemption**
   - No federal income tax on research operations
   - State tax exemptions in most jurisdictions
   - Property tax exemptions where applicable

4. **Credibility**
   - IRS-recognized charitable status
   - Increased donor confidence
   - Academic partnership opportunities

5. **Perpetual Existence**
   - Survives beyond any individual
   - Institutional knowledge preservation
   - Long-term research continuity

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Formation & Status

**Q: When will 501(c)(3) status be official?**  
A: Expected Q1 2025. Operations beginning now under fiscal sponsor until approved.

**Q: Can I donate before 501(c)(3) approval?**  
A: Yes, through our fiscal sponsor. Donations will be tax-deductible retroactively.

**Q: What if 501(c)(3) application is denied?**  
A: Unlikely given mission alignment, but we'd restructure and reapply. All donations refundable if desired.

### Financial

**Q: How much money goes to actual research?**  
A: Target 85% to mission (research + infrastructure), 15% to administration.

**Q: Can I specify how my donation is used?**  
A: Yes, restricted donations accepted for specific bounties or projects.

**Q: Are cryptocurrency donations accepted?**  
A: Yes, and they're tax-deductible at fair market value on donation date.

### Operations

**Q: Who owns the institute?**  
A: No one. Nonprofits don't have owners. Governed by board for public benefit.

**Q: Can the institute be sold?**  
A: No. Assets must remain in charitable use. If dissolved, assets go to another 501(c)(3).

**Q: What oversight exists?**  
A: IRS oversight, state charity bureau, public disclosure requirements, board governance.
