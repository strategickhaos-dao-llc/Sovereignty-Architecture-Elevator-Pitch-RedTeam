## üí° Frequently Asked Questions

### Filing Process

**Q: Do I need a lawyer?**  
A: No. Provisional patent applications are designed for pro se (self) filing.

**Q: What if I make a mistake?**  
A: The filing date is preserved. You can submit corrections if needed.

**Q: How long does USPTO processing take?**  
A: You get a confirmation within hours. Official receipt arrives in 1-7 days.

### Legal Impact

**Q: Does this actually stop patent trolls?**  
A: Yes. USPTO filing is one of the strongest forms of defensive prior art.

**Q: What if someone already filed a similar patent?**  
A: If after this date, your filing defeats theirs. If before, both exist as prior art.

**Q: Is this legally binding?**  
A: Yes. Patent examiners must consider provisional applications as prior art.

### Cost & Maintenance

**Q: Are there ongoing fees?**  
A: No. The $75 is one-time. Let the application expire after 12 months.

**Q: Can I convert to a full patent?**  
A: Yes, but not recommended. The goal is defensive prior art, not exclusive rights.

**Q: What if I can't afford $75?**  
A: Public disclosure in this repo still creates prior art, just without official USPTO record.
