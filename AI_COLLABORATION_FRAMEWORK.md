### What AI Systems CANNOT Do

AI systems cannot legally or academically:
- ‚ùå Hold authorship on papers, patents, or copyrighted works
- ‚ùå Be listed as inventors or co-authors
- ‚ùå Own intellectual property rights
- ‚ùå Make scientific claims independently
- ‚ùå Be held legally responsible for content

**Why this matters:** Violating these principles can result in:
- Paper rejection by academic journals
- Patent invalidation
- Copyright disputes
- Loss of legal protection for your work

### What AI-Assisted Work CAN Include

AI collaboration can be properly documented as:
- ‚úÖ AI-assisted drafting or editing
- ‚úÖ Model-guided exploration and ideation
- ‚úÖ Cognitive scaffolding via LLM dialog
- ‚úÖ Generative model used as thought-partner
- ‚úÖ Socratic guidance using LLMs as conceptual mirrors
- ‚úÖ Iterative reasoning support through prompt-response cycles

## üî¨ Methodology: Human-LLM Collaboration Process

### The Cognitive Scaffolding Model

**Definition:** Human-LLM collaboration where the AI serves as an external 
working memory and thought amplifier, enabling:

1. **Ideation Expansion**
   - Human provides core concept or problem
   - LLM generates multiple perspectives and approaches
   - Human selects, refines, and synthesizes results

2. **Socratic Dialog**
   - Human explores ideas through conversational iteration
   - LLM asks clarifying questions and identifies gaps
   - Human resolves ambiguities and makes decisions

3. **Structured Decomposition**
   - Human presents complex problem
   - LLM breaks down into manageable components
   - Human validates and adjusts decomposition

4. **Pattern Recognition and Classification**
   - Human shares unstructured thoughts or data
   - LLM identifies patterns and suggests taxonomies
   - Human validates patterns and applies to work

5. **Iterative Refinement**
   - Human creates initial draft or solution
   - LLM suggests improvements and identifies issues
   - Human evaluates and selectively incorporates feedback

### Research-Grade Process Documentation

When documenting AI-assisted work for academic purposes, include:

**Methodology Section Example:**
```markdown
### AI-Assisted Development Methodology

This work employed a human-LLM collaborative methodology:

1. **Initial Ideation:** Core concepts and research questions were developed
   by the human author through dialog with GPT-4 for perspective expansion.

2. **Framework Development:** The author used AI-assisted brainstorming to
   explore architectural patterns, with all final selections made by the
   human author based on domain expertise.

3. **Documentation Refinement:** Draft documentation was iteratively refined
   through AI-assisted editing for clarity and structure, with the author
   maintaining full editorial control and factual accuracy responsibility.

4. **Code Implementation:** Programming was conducted by the human author
   with AI-powered code completion and suggestion tools (GitHub Copilot),
   with all code reviewed and validated by the author before integration.

LLM interactions served as cognitive scaffolding rather than autonomous
development, with the human author maintaining complete agency over all
technical and creative decisions.
```

## üß† Cognitive Process Frameworks

### Framework 1: Bloom's Taxonomy Applied to AI Collaboration

**How AI assists at each cognitive level:**

| Bloom Level | Human Role | AI Role |
|------------|------------|---------|
| **Remember** | Recall goals and constraints | Provide context and references |
| **Understand** | Comprehend implications | Explain concepts and generate examples |
| **Apply** | Make implementation decisions | Suggest patterns and approaches |
| **Analyze** | Evaluate options critically | Break down complexity, identify patterns |
| **Evaluate** | Judge quality and correctness | Offer multiple perspectives for comparison |
| **Create** | Synthesize novel solutions | Generate variations and combinations |

**Key Insight:** The human operates at ALL levels, while AI assists primarily 
at lower-to-middle levels (Remember, Understand, Analyze). Final decisions 
(Evaluate, Create) remain human responsibilities.

### Framework 2: Neurodivergent Cognitive Scaffolding

**Special considerations for neurodivergent thinkers:**

AI collaboration can be particularly powerful for:
- **ADHD:** External working memory and thought organization
- **Autism:** Pattern recognition and systematic decomposition
- **Dyslexia:** Text structuring and formatting assistance
- **Hyperfocus:** Enabling extended creative sessions with instant feedback

**Acknowledgment Language:**
```markdown
The author utilized AI-assisted cognitive scaffolding to support 
neurodivergent thinking patterns, enabling more efficient translation 
of associative ideation into structured technical documentation.
```

## üõ°Ô∏è Ethical Considerations

### Transparency Principles

1. **Disclose AI Use:** Always acknowledge when AI tools contributed to your work
2. **Specify Role:** Clarify what the AI did vs. what you decided
3. **Take Responsibility:** Own all claims, errors, and decisions
4. **Respect Guidelines:** Follow your field's specific AI disclosure policies

### Avoiding Common Pitfalls

**DON'T:**
- ‚ùå Present AI-generated content as purely human-authored
- ‚ùå Use AI to obscure lack of domain knowledge
- ‚ùå Rely on AI for factual claims without verification
- ‚ùå List AI as co-author or contributor

**DO:**
- ‚úÖ Verify all AI-suggested facts and references
- ‚úÖ Apply critical thinking to all AI outputs
- ‚úÖ Use AI to enhance your expertise, not replace it
- ‚úÖ Acknowledge AI assistance transparently

### Institutional Guidelines

Many institutions now have AI use policies. Common requirements:
- Statement of AI tool usage in papers/theses
- Description of how AI was used (specific tasks)
- Confirmation that human reviewed and takes responsibility for content
- Citation of AI tools in methods or acknowledgments section

**Always check your institution's specific requirements.**

## üí¨ Questions and Guidance

### "Can I acknowledge AI help without looking less credible?"

**Absolutely yes.** Transparency about tools used is scientifically rigorous 
and ethically correct. Using AI effectively demonstrates:
- Understanding of modern development practices
- Ability to leverage available tools
- Intellectual honesty and transparency
- Commitment to reproducible methodology

### "How much AI assistance is 'too much'?"

There's no fixed threshold, but ask:
- Am I learning and growing, or just copy-pasting?
- Could I defend and explain this work without the AI?
- Do I understand the domain well enough to evaluate AI outputs?
- Am I using AI to enhance my thinking or replace it?

If you're learning and maintaining expertise, you're in the right zone.

### "What if my field doesn't have AI disclosure guidelines yet?"

Be a pioneer! Use the templates in this document and help establish norms 
for your field. Future researchers will thank you for leading with 
transparency.
