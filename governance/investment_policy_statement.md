# Investment Policy Statement (IPS) for SNHU Ecosystem Fund

**Effective Date:** December 1, 2025  
**Adopted by Board of Directors on:** November 22, 2025  
**Next Review Date:** December 1, 2026 (and annually thereafter)

---

## 1. Purpose

This Investment Policy Statement (IPS) provides guidelines for the prudent investment and management of the SNHU Ecosystem Fund's financial assets in support of our mission:  
**advancing open-source educational tools, student success resources, and nonprofit innovation inspired by the Southern New Hampshire University ecosystem.**

The IPS ensures stewardship is prudent, values-aligned (with strong ESG and education-focus preferences), and balances long-term growth with liquidity needs.

---

## 2. Scope of Assets

- **Covered:** All investable assets including unrestricted reserves, future endowment funds, and any donor-restricted investment funds.
- **Excluded:** Day-to-day operating cash held in checking/savings accounts for immediate expenses (<90 days).

---

## 3. Investment Objectives

- **Return Expectations:** Target a real (inflation-adjusted) total return of 5–7% annually, net of fees, over rolling 5–10 year periods.
- **Risk Tolerance:** Moderate; prioritize capital preservation while accepting moderate equity volatility.
- **Liquidity:** Maintain 20–30% in highly liquid assets sufficient to cover at least 12 months of projected operating expenses.
- **Mission Alignment:** Favor ESG-screened investments; explicitly exclude tobacco, weapons, predatory lending, and fossil-fuel-heavy companies.

---

## 4. Asset Allocation

| Asset Class          | Target % | Allowable Range % | Examples                                      |
|----------------------|----------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Equities/ETFs        | 55%      | 40–70%            | VTI, VXUS, SPY, ESGU (ESG U.S. equity)        |
| Fixed Income         | 35%      | 20–50%            | BND, TIP, municipal bonds, U.S. Treasuries    |
| Alternatives/Cash    | 10%      | 5–30%             | Money-market funds, CDs; max 10% in stock-index futures via NinjaTrader-linked broker (for analysis/hedging only) |

Portfolio will be rebalanced annually or when any asset class deviates more than 5% from target.

---

## 5. Permissible Investments

**Allowed**
- Publicly traded stocks and ETFs
- Mutual funds and index funds
- U.S. government and investment-grade corporate/municipal bonds
- Stock-index futures contracts (for hedging only, executed via approved broker)
- Cash equivalents and CDs

**Prohibited**
- Individual private company shares
- Real estate (direct ownership)
- Cryptocurrencies (outside regulated futures)
- High-leverage or speculative derivatives
- Any investment conflicting with our mission or ESG screens

**Platforms**
- Primary custody and execution: nonprofit brokerage account at Fidelity, Interactive Brokers, or Charles Schwab
- NinjaTrader: permitted solely for market analysis, backtesting, and educational/simulation purposes

---

## 6. Governance and Responsibilities

- **Investment Committee** (or full Board if no committee yet): Approves IPS, selects brokerage relationships, reviews performance quarterly.
- **Delegated Manager**: Treasurer (currently [Your Name]) or appointed external RIA acting as fiduciary.
- **Conflict of Interest**: Any board or staff member with a material financial interest in a proposed investment must disclose and recuse themselves from voting.

---

## 7. Monitoring & Reporting

- **Primary Benchmark**: 55/35/10 blend of MSCI ACWI IMI ESG Leaders Index, Bloomberg U.S. Aggregate Bond Index, and 90-day T-Bill.
- **Reporting**: Quarterly performance reports to the Board; annual IPS review and update.
- **Spending Policy**: Up to 5% of the trailing 12-quarter average portfolio balance may be withdrawn annually for operations (subject to board approval).

---

## 8. Compliance & Amendments

All investment activities shall comply with the Uniform Prudent Management of Institutional Funds Act (UPMIFA), IRS nonprofit regulations, and the organization's bylaws. This IPS may be amended by majority vote of the Board of Directors.
