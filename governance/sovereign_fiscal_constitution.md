## Preamble

We, the Members and Board of [Organization Name], in order to establish fiscal sovereignty, ensure financial sustainability, promote radical transparency, protect donor trust, and secure the perpetual operation of our mission, do hereby establish this Sovereign Fiscal Constitution as the supreme financial governance document of our organization.

This Constitution shall govern all financial decisions, transactions, and policies, superseding all prior financial policies except where explicitly stated otherwise or where in conflict with federal or state law.

## Article I: Foundational Principles

### Section 1: Core Values

**1.1 Fiscal Sovereignty**  
The organization shall maintain complete control over its financial destiny through:
- Decentralized treasury management
- Blockchain-verified transactions
- Multi-signature controls
- Independence from external financial coercion

**1.2 Radical Transparency**  
All financial activities shall be:
- Recorded on blockchain within 24 hours
- Publicly verifiable (except donor PII)
- Auditable by any stakeholder
- Permanently preserved

**1.3 Sustainability First**  
Financial decisions shall prioritize:
- Long-term mission continuity
- Intergenerational equity
- Reserve adequacy
- Sustainable growth

**1.4 Donor Supremacy**  
Donor intent and trust shall be:
- Scrupulously honored
- Privacy-protected by default
- Acknowledged transparently
- Reported accurately

**1.5 Algorithmic Accountability**  
Financial governance shall be:
- Rule-based and predictable
- Smart contract-enforced where possible
- Automatically auditable
- Resistant to individual manipulation

## Article III: Revenue Framework

### Section 1: Revenue Principles

**3.1.1 Diversification Mandate**  
No single revenue source shall exceed [40]% of total annual revenue, calculated on a rolling 3-year average.

**Categories:**
- Individual donations
- Institutional grants
- Earned revenue (program fees, etc.)
- Investment income
- In-kind contributions

**3.1.2 Sustainability Target**  
Minimum [60]% of annual revenue shall come from recurring or predictable sources:
- Monthly donors
- Multi-year grants
- Endowment income
- Membership fees
- Subscription services

**3.1.3 Growth Target**  
Target annual revenue growth: [5-10]%, adjusted for:
- Economic conditions
- Mission priorities
- Strategic opportunities
- Sustainability considerations

### Section 2: Acceptable Revenue Sources

**3.2.1 Permitted Sources**
- Individual contributions
- Corporate/foundation grants (mission-aligned)
- Government grants (no political strings)
- Earned income (mission-related)
- Investment returns
- Bequests and planned gifts
- Cryptocurrency donations
- In-kind contributions (properly valued)

**3.2.2 Prohibited Sources**
- Political campaign contributions
- Illegal activities
- Activities undermining mission
- Exploitative practices
- Predatory lending
- Sources violating 501(c)(3) rules

**3.2.3 Conditional Acceptance**  
The following require Board approval:
- Restricted donations > $[X],000
- Naming rights agreements
- Sponsorships with branding
- Government contracts with restrictions
- Cryptocurrency > $[Y],000 (valuation complexity)

### Section 3: Donation Policies

**3.3.1 Gift Acceptance**  
Standard acceptance criteria:
- Mission alignment
- No undue restrictions
- Legal and ethical
- Properly valued
- Acknowledgeable

**3.3.2 Cryptocurrency Donations**  
Accepted with conditions:
- Convert to fiat within [X] business days (unless policy allows holding)
- Fair market value at donation time
- Transaction recorded on blockchain
- Tax documentation provided
- Compliance with IRS Notice 2014-21

**3.3.3 Donor Privacy**  
All donor information shall be:
- SHA-3 hashed with UUID salt
- Stored with minimal PII
- Never sold or shared
- Opt-in communications only
- Deletable upon request (except legal requirements)

## Article IV: Expenditure Framework

### Section 1: Expenditure Principles

**4.1.1 Mission First**  
All expenditures shall demonstrably advance the organization's mission or enable operations essential to mission delivery.

**4.1.2 Cost Efficiency**  
The organization shall seek maximum mission impact per dollar spent, through:
- Competitive procurement
- Bulk purchasing where appropriate
- Technology leverage
- Volunteer engagement
- Strategic partnerships

**4.1.3 Transparency**  
All expenditures shall be:
- Recorded in accounting system
- Posted to blockchain within 24 hours
- Categorized properly (program/admin/fundraising)
- Documented with receipts/invoices
- Auditable by stakeholders

### Section 2: Spending Allocations

**4.2.1 Program Services Minimum**  
Minimum [70]% of total annual expenditures shall be for program services directly advancing the mission.

*Calculation: Program expenses / Total expenses*

**4.2.2 Administrative Maximum**  
Maximum [20]% of total annual expenditures shall be for general administrative overhead.

*Included: Executive salaries, office, accounting, legal, insurance, IT infrastructure*

**4.2.3 Fundraising Maximum**  
Maximum [15]% of total annual expenditures shall be for fundraising activities.

*Calculation: Fundraising expenses / Total expenses*  
*Note: Program + Admin + Fundraising may exceed 100% due to rounding or allocation methodology*

**4.2.4 Efficiency Ratio Minimum**  
Target efficiency ratio: [85]%+  
*Calculation: Program expenses / Total expenses*

**4.2.5 Fundraising Efficiency**  
Target: Raise $[X] per $1 spent on fundraising  
*Calculation: Contributions raised / Fundraising expenses*

### Section 3: Authorization Levels

**4.3.1 Spending Authority Tiers**

| Amount | Authorized By | Signatures Required | Timelock |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|----------|
| < $1,000 | Staff/Officers | 1 | None |
| $1,000 - $5,000 | Treasurer or ED | 1 | None |
| $5,000 - $25,000 | Treasurer + ED | 2 | None |
| $25,000 - $100,000 | Board Treasurer Committee | 2 of 3 | 24 hours |
| $100,000 - $500,000 | Board Vote | 3 of 5 multi-sig | 72 hours |
| > $500,000 | Supermajority Board + Member Vote | 4 of 6 multi-sig | 7 days |

**4.3.2 Emergency Expenditures**  
In case of genuine emergency (immediate threat to life, property, or mission):
- ED may authorize up to $[X],000
- Must notify Board within 24 hours
- Must provide full documentation within 72 hours
- Board may override or ratify

**4.3.3 Prohibited Expenditures**  
The following are absolutely prohibited:
- Political campaign contributions or expenditures
- Private benefit to insiders (except reasonable compensation)
- Illegal activities
- Excessive compensation
- Luxury items not mission-related
- Lobbying beyond 501(h) safe harbor
- Expenditures violating donor restrictions

## Article V: Reserve and Sustainability

### Section 1: Operating Reserve

**5.1.1 Reserve Requirement**  
The organization shall maintain an Operating Reserve equal to [6-12] months of average operating expenses.

*Calculation: (Sum of last 12 months expenses) / 12 Ã— [6-12]*

**5.1.2 Reserve Purpose**  
The Operating Reserve exists to:
- Ensure continuity during revenue disruptions
- Provide working capital
- Cover unexpected expenses
- Maintain donor confidence
- Enable strategic opportunities

**5.1.3 Reserve Investment**  
Operating Reserve shall be invested:
- Liquid or near-liquid instruments
- Low risk (investment grade)
- Accessible within [30] days
- Diversified across institutions
- FDIC insured where applicable

**5.1.4 Reserve Usage**  
Operating Reserve may be used for:
- Revenue shortfalls (replacement plan required)
- Unexpected major expenses
- Strategic opportunities (Board supermajority)
- Emergency situations

Reserve may NOT be used for:
- Routine operations (if reserve is within target)
- Speculative ventures
- Politically motivated expenditures

**5.1.5 Reserve Replenishment**  
If Operating Reserve falls below minimum:
- Budget must prioritize rebuilding reserve
- [X]% of surplus revenue directed to reserve
- Fundraising may be designated for reserve
- Timeline: Restore within [X] months

### Section 2: Program Reserve

**5.2.1 Purpose**  
Program Reserves may be established for:
- Multi-year program commitments
- Capital projects
- Planned expansions
- Designated initiatives

**5.2.2 Board Designation**  
Program Reserves require:
- Board resolution designating amount and purpose
- Clear timeline and conditions
- Annual review and reaffirmation
- Release conditions specified

### Section 3: Endowment (if applicable)

**5.3.1 Endowment Establishment**  
A permanent endowment may be established by:
- Board resolution (supermajority)
- Member vote ([X]% approval)
- Donor-restricted gifts
- Bequest designations

**5.3.2 Endowment Spending Policy**  
Annual spending from endowment:
- Rate: [4-5]% of trailing [3]-year average balance
- Calculation: December 31 values for prior 3 years
- Inflation adjustment considered
- Mission priorities guide allocation

**5.3.3 Endowment Investment Policy**  
- Time horizon: Perpetual
- Return target: [CPI + X]% over rolling 10 years
- Risk tolerance: Moderate
- Diversification: Across asset classes
- Socially responsible: Mission-aligned values
- Professional management: Recommended above $[X] million

### Section 1: Debt Limitations

**8.1.1 Borrowing Restrictions**  
The organization may NOT incur debt exceeding:
- [25]% of net assets, OR
- $[X],000, whichever is less

**8.1.2 Permitted Debt**

- Line of credit (operational cash flow)

**8.1.3 Prohibited Debt**

- Credit card debt (except paid monthly)
- Payday or predatory loans
- Personal guarantees by board members
- Debt for speculation
- Debt exceeding constitutional limits

### Section 2: Debt Authorization

**8.2.1 Board Approval Required**  
All debt requires:
- Board resolution (supermajority)
- Repayment plan
- Constitutional compliance verification
- Member notification
- Legal review

**8.2.2 Member Override**  
Members may override Board debt authorization:
- [X]% vote within [Y] days of notification
- Blockchain-recorded votes
- Rationale provided

### Section 3: Debt Management

**8.3.1 Debt Service Coverage**  
Organization shall maintain:
- Debt service coverage ratio > [1.5x]
- *Calculation: (Revenue - Expenses + Depreciation) / Debt Service*

**8.3.2 Prepayment**  
Excess funds should be used to:
1. Build operating reserve to target
2. Prepay debt (if no penalty)
3. Fund program reserves
4. Build endowment

- Comparable to similar organizations

**9.1.2 Comparability**
