# Royalty Oversight and Financial Governance Policy
## Board Oversight of Technology Royalties and NinjaTrader Dividends

**Effective Date**: 2025-11-23  
**Policy Owner**: Board of Directors  
**Review Frequency**: Annual

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes board-level governance and oversight procedures for technology royalty arrangements, including NinjaTrader dividends, to ensure appropriate legal, financial, and mission-aligned management of these revenue streams.

## Scope

This policy applies to all royalty arrangements, licensing agreements, and similar income-generating intellectual property transactions entered into by Strategickhaos DAO LLC / Valoryield Engine.

---

## Board Responsibilities

### Oversight and Approval

#### Required Board Approval

The Board of Directors must approve:

1. **New Royalty Agreements**
   - Any new licensing or royalty arrangement
   - Material amendments to existing agreements
   - Agreements expected to generate >$10,000 annually or >10% of organizational revenue
   
2. **IP Strategy Decisions**
   - Overall intellectual property strategy
   - Significant IP asset acquisitions or disposals
   - IP protection and enforcement actions

3. **Financial Policies**
   - Royalty revenue allocation policies
   - Reserve fund policies related to royalty income
   - Investment of royalty-derived funds

#### Delegation Authority

The Board may delegate to the Executive Director:
- Routine administration of approved agreements
- Minor amendments not affecting payment terms
- Operational decisions within board-approved strategy
- Agreements under $10,000 annual value (with quarterly reporting)

### Fiduciary Duties

#### Duty of Care
Board members must:
- Stay informed about royalty arrangements and their impact
- Review financial reports including royalty revenue
- Ask questions and seek clarification on complex arrangements
- Attend meetings where royalty matters are discussed
- Engage appropriate expertise when needed

#### Duty of Loyalty
Board members must:
- Act in the organization's best interest, not personal interest
- Disclose any conflicts of interest related to royalty arrangements
- Recuse themselves from decisions where conflicts exist
- Not use organizational IP for personal gain

#### Duty of Obedience
Board members must ensure:
- Royalty arrangements advance the nonprofit's mission
- Compliance with laws and regulations
- Proper use of royalty income for exempt purposes
- Adherence to organizational governing documents

```yaml

    - "Payment timeliness and any delays"
    
  trend_analysis:
    - "Year-over-year trends"
    - "Seasonal patterns"
    - "Revenue concentration risk"
    - "Variance explanations"
    
  risk_assessment:
    - "Dependency risk (% of total revenue)"
    - "Counterparty financial health"
    - "Agreement expiration dates"
    - "Market conditions affecting IP value"
    
  forward_looking:
    - "Updated revenue projections"
    - "Upcoming renewal decisions"
    - "New opportunities or threats"
```

#### Annual Comprehensive Review

The Board shall conduct an annual comprehensive review including:
- Full audit of all royalty arrangements
- Legal compliance review
- Tax treatment verification (UBIT analysis)
- Market valuation of IP assets
- Strategic assessment of IP portfolio

### Budget Oversight

#### Revenue Budgeting
- Conservative revenue projections for royalty income
- Documented assumptions and risk factors
- Scenario planning (optimistic, expected, pessimistic)
- Board approval of annual revenue budget

#### Expense Management
- Costs associated with IP maintenance and protection
- Legal fees for agreement management
- Audit and compliance costs
- Board approval of IP-related expenses >$5,000

## Legal and Compliance Oversight

### Agreement Review Process

#### Pre-Approval Review

Before presenting any royalty agreement to the Board:

1. **Legal Review**
   - IP counsel reviews all terms
   - Identifies legal risks and protections needed
   - Confirms alignment with nonprofit requirements
   - Written legal opinion provided to Board

2. **Financial Analysis**
   - Pro forma revenue projections
   - Tax implications (UBIT analysis)
   - Budget impact assessment
   - Comparison to market standards

3. **Mission Alignment**
   - Documentation of how IP supports mission
   - Explanation of public benefit
   - Assessment of private benefit risk
   - Consistency with exempt purpose

#### Board Review Criteria

The Board shall evaluate proposed agreements on:

```markdown
## Royalty Agreement Evaluation Criteria

### Financial Viability
- [ ] Revenue projections are realistic and well-supported
- [ ] Payment terms are favorable and enforceable
- [ ] Counterparty financial stability is adequate
- [ ] Agreement includes minimum payment guarantees (preferred)
- [ ] Audit rights are included

### Legal Protection
- [ ] Strong contract provisions protect organization
- [ ] IP ownership is clearly established
- [ ] Termination provisions are fair
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanisms are specified
- [ ] Agreement has been reviewed by qualified counsel

### Tax Compliance
- [ ] Income likely qualifies as royalty (not UBIT)
- [ ] Organization minimally involved (passive income)
- [ ] Private benefit concerns have been addressed
- [ ] Arm's length transaction terms verified

### Risk Management
- [ ] Does not create excessive revenue concentration
- [ ] Reputational risks have been considered
- [ ] Exit strategy is documented
- [ ] Insurance or other protections are adequate

### Mission Alignment
- [ ] IP developed as part of exempt purpose
- [ ] Public benefit is clear and substantial
- [ ] No impermissible private benefit
- [ ] Advances organizational mission
```

### Conflict of Interest Management

#### Disclosure Requirements

Any board member, officer, or key employee must disclose:
- Personal or family interest in potential licensee
- Financial interest in related technologies
- Business relationships with counterparties
- Any other potential conflicts

#### Recusal Procedures

When conflicts exist:
1. Full disclosure to Board prior to discussion
2. Conflicted individual may present facts but must recuse from discussion and vote
3. Board decision made by conflict-free directors
4. Documentation of conflict and recusal in minutes
5. Annual conflict of interest statement from all board members

## Risk Management

### Board Risk Oversight

#### Risk Committee Responsibilities

The Board (or designated Risk Committee) shall:

1. **Identify Risks**
   - Review [THREAT_MODEL.md](../THREAT_MODEL.md) quarterly
   - Assess new and emerging risks
   - Evaluate risk interdependencies
   - Update risk register

2. **Assess Risks**
   - Likelihood and impact analysis
   - Risk prioritization
   - Tolerance level determination
   - Cost-benefit analysis of mitigation

3. **Mitigate Risks**
   - Approve risk mitigation strategies
   - Allocate resources for risk management
   - Monitor implementation of controls
   - Review effectiveness regularly

4. **Monitor Risks**
   - Regular risk reporting to full Board
   - Key risk indicators tracking
   - Incident and near-miss review
   - Continuous risk environment scanning

#### Key Royalty Risks for Board Oversight

```yaml
priority_risks:
  payment_interruption:
    oversight: "Monthly payment monitoring report to Treasurer"
    escalation: "Board notification within 24 hours if payment late"
    
  revenue_concentration:
    oversight: "Quarterly review of revenue mix"
    action_trigger: "Any single source exceeds 40% of revenue"
    
  legal_challenges:
    oversight: "Immediate board notification of any legal threat"
    response: "Emergency meeting within 48 hours if needed"
    
  tax_compliance:
    oversight: "Annual review with tax counsel"
    documentation: "UBIT analysis in board materials annually"
```

### Insurance Coverage

#### Required Coverage

The Board shall ensure adequate insurance:

1. **Directors and Officers (D&O) Insurance**
   - Minimum coverage: $1,000,000
   - Coverage includes IP licensing decisions
   - Annual review of adequacy

2. **Professional Liability Insurance**
   - Coverage for AI operations and consulting services
   - IP-related claims coverage
   - Minimum coverage: $1,000,000

3. **Cyber Insurance**
   - Data breach coverage
   - Business interruption coverage
   - Minimum coverage: $500,000

4. **General Liability Insurance**
   - Comprehensive coverage
   - Minimum coverage: $2,000,000

#### Annual Insurance Review

The Board shall annually:
- Review all insurance policies
- Assess coverage adequacy
- Compare to industry standards
- Approve insurance budget

## Decision-Making Framework

### Materiality Thresholds

```yaml
materiality_framework:
  board_approval_required:
    - "Royalty agreements >$10,000 annual value"
    - "Agreements >10% of projected revenue"
    - "Any exclusive licensing arrangements"
    - "Multi-year agreements (>3 years)"
    - "Amendments to payment terms"
    
  board_notification_required:
    - "New agreements $5,000-$10,000"
    - "Material communications from licensees"
    - "Payment delays >15 days"
    - "Any disputes or legal matters"
    
  executive_authority:
    - "Routine agreement administration"
    - "Agreements <$5,000 annual value"
    - "Non-material amendments"
    - "Routine communications"
```

### Board Meeting Requirements

#### Regular Meetings
- Royalty revenue on standing agenda
- Quarterly financial reports including royalty analysis
- Annual comprehensive IP portfolio review

#### Special Meetings
- Called for major royalty decisions
- Emergency response to payment interruption
- Legal threats or significant disputes
- Strategic IP decisions

### Documentation Requirements

#### Board Minutes

Board minutes shall document:
- All royalty-related decisions and votes
- Rationale for decisions
- Conflicts disclosed and recusals
- Risk assessments and mitigations
- Future action items and deadlines

#### Board Materials

Board packets shall include:
- Executive summaries of complex agreements
- Financial analysis and projections
- Legal opinions (redacted if privileged)
- Risk assessments
- Recommendations from management

## Audit and Accountability

### Internal Audit

#### Annual Royalty Audit

The Board shall ensure annual internal audit covering:

```markdown
## Royalty Management Internal Audit Checklist

### Agreement Compliance
- [ ] All agreements properly authorized and executed
- [ ] Payment terms being followed by both parties
- [ ] Audit rights exercised where beneficial
- [ ] Required reports and communications completed

### Financial Management
- [ ] All royalty income properly recorded
- [ ] Dedicated account maintained correctly
- [ ] Reserve fund at required levels
- [ ] Budget variance explanations documented

### Legal and Tax Compliance
- [ ] Income properly classified on Form 990
- [ ] UBIT analysis current and accurate
- [ ] No private benefit or inurement
- [ ] All legal requirements met

### Risk Management
- [ ] Risk assessments current
- [ ] Insurance coverage adequate
- [ ] Contingency plans tested
- [ ] Board oversight procedures followed
```

### External Audit

#### Independent Financial Audit

Annual independent audit shall include:
- Review of royalty revenue recognition
- Testing of internal controls over royalty income
- Verification of reserve fund management
- Compliance with board policies
- Opinion on financial statement accuracy

#### Tax Compliance Review

Annual review by qualified tax counsel:
- UBIT analysis for all royalty income
- Form 990 preparation and review
- State tax compliance verification
- Documentation of tax positions

### Whistleblower and Accountability

#### Reporting Mechanisms

The Board shall ensure:
- Whistleblower policy protecting reporters
- Anonymous reporting channel available
- Board audit committee receives all reports
- Investigations conducted independently
- Findings reported to full board

#### Board Self-Assessment

Annual board self-assessment including:
- Effectiveness of royalty oversight
- Adequacy of financial reporting
- Quality of risk management
- Board education needs
- Policy compliance

## Policy Administration

### Policy Review and Updates

**Review Schedule**: Annual, or more frequently if:
- Significant change in royalty arrangements
- Legal or regulatory changes
- Material risk events
- Best practice evolution

**Review Process**:
1. Finance Committee reviews policy annually
2. Recommendations presented to full Board
3. Board discussion and approval of changes
4. Updated policy distributed to all relevant parties

### Training and Education

#### Board Orientation

New board members shall receive training on:
- Fiduciary duties related to royalty income
- Organization's IP portfolio and strategy
- Financial oversight responsibilities
- Risk management framework
- Relevant policies and procedures

#### Continuing Education

Annual board education including:
- Updates on royalty performance
- Changes in legal or tax landscape
- Emerging risks and opportunities
- Best practices in nonprofit IP management

### Policy Violations

Violations of this policy shall be:
- Reported to Board President immediately
- Investigated by independent party
- Addressed through appropriate corrective action
- Documented in board records
- Used to improve policies and procedures
