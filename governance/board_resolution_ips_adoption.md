# Board Resolution: Adoption of Investment Policy Statement

**SNHU Ecosystem Fund**  
**Board of Directors Meeting**  
**Date:** November 22, 2025

---

## RESOLUTION NO. 2025-11

### ADOPTION OF INVESTMENT POLICY STATEMENT

**WHEREAS**, the SNHU Ecosystem Fund (the "Organization") is a nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing open-source educational tools, student success resources, and nonprofit innovation inspired by the Southern New Hampshire University ecosystem; and

**WHEREAS**, the Board of Directors recognizes the importance of establishing prudent investment policies to manage the Organization's financial assets in accordance with the Uniform Prudent Management of Institutional Funds Act (UPMIFA) and applicable IRS regulations; and

**WHEREAS**, the Board has reviewed the proposed Investment Policy Statement (IPS) dated December 1, 2025, which establishes guidelines for asset allocation, risk management, ESG screening, and fiduciary oversight; and

**WHEREAS**, the Board believes that adoption of the IPS will enhance the Organization's financial stewardship and ensure alignment with its mission and values;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED** that:

1. **Adoption of IPS**: The Board of Directors hereby adopts the Investment Policy Statement dated December 1, 2025, effective immediately.

2. **Investment Committee**: The Board authorizes the formation of an Investment Committee (or delegates these responsibilities to the full Board until such committee is formed) to:
   - Implement the IPS guidelines
   - Select and monitor brokerage relationships
   - Review investment performance quarterly
   - Recommend amendments to the IPS as needed

3. **Treasurer Authority**: The Treasurer is authorized to:
   - Open and manage brokerage accounts in accordance with the IPS
   - Execute investment transactions within the approved asset allocation ranges
   - Engage external investment advisors as needed, subject to Board approval
   - Prepare quarterly investment reports for Board review

4. **Brokerage Selection**: The Treasurer is authorized to establish brokerage relationships with one or more of the following institutions: Fidelity Investments, Interactive Brokers, or Charles Schwab, subject to favorable terms for nonprofit accounts.

5. **Annual Review**: The Investment Committee (or full Board) shall review the IPS annually and recommend updates as needed to reflect changes in the Organization's financial position, market conditions, or regulatory requirements.

6. **Conflict of Interest**: All Board members and staff involved in investment decisions shall comply with the Organization's conflict of interest policy and disclose any material financial interests in proposed investments.

7. **Documentation**: The Secretary is directed to:
   - Attach a copy of this Resolution to the signed IPS
   - Provide copies to the Treasurer and any external investment advisors
   - Maintain these documents in the Organization's official records
