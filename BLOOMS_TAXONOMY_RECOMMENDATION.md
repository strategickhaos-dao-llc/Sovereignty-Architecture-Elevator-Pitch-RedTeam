## **Usage Instructions for Faculty**

### **How to Use This Template:**

1. **Select Appropriate Version**: Choose standard paragraph, extended version, or targeted variation based on recommendation context

2. **Customize with Specifics**: Replace bracketed placeholders with:
   - Specific courses or projects where you observed the student
   - Concrete examples of their work
   - Particular achievements or contributions
   - Your relationship to the student (instructor, advisor, research supervisor)

3. **Add Personal Observations**: Include specific incidents or examples that illustrate:
   - Analytical work you observed
   - Evaluation frameworks they developed
   - Creative solutions they generated
   - Impact of their contributions

4. **Verify Claims**: Ensure all statements are:
   - Based on your direct observation
   - Supported by concrete evidence
   - Consistent with documented work
   - Appropriate for the recommendation context

5. **Include Contact Information**: Provide your title, institution, contact details for follow-up questions

### **Evidence to Reference:**

* **GitHub Repositories**: Specific projects demonstrating Create tier work
* **Course Projects**: Examples of analytical or evaluative work
* **Research Contributions**: Papers, presentations, or innovations
* **Technical Presentations**: Demonstrations of understanding
* **Peer Collaboration**: Team projects showing cognitive capabilities
* **Independent Work**: Self-directed projects indicating autonomy

## **Bloom's Taxonomy Quick Reference for Faculty**

### **Analyze Tier Indicators:**
* Breaks systems into components
* Identifies patterns and relationships
* Traces data flows and dependencies
* Compares and contrasts alternatives
* Diagnoses problems systematically

### **Evaluate Tier Indicators:**
* Assesses quality against criteria
* Critiques designs and approaches
* Justifies decisions with evidence
* Weighs tradeoffs systematically
* Considers ethical implications

### **Create Tier Indicators:**
* Designs original solutions
* Synthesizes cross-domain knowledge
* Invents new tools or frameworks
* Architects novel systems
* Generates innovative approaches
