## üéØ Key Principles (Remember These!)

### ‚úÖ DO:
- Disclose AI tool usage transparently
- Specify what AI did vs. what you decided
- Verify all AI-generated facts
- Take full responsibility for content
- Follow your institution's guidelines

### ‚ùå DON'T:
- List AI as co-author or inventor
- Present AI content as purely human-authored
- Use AI to replace domain expertise
- Rely on AI without verification
- Hide AI assistance

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Start Early**: Don't wait until submission to think about AI acknowledgment
2. **Be Specific**: "Used ChatGPT for ideation" is better than "used AI"
3. **Be Honest**: Over-disclose rather than under-disclose
4. **Be Consistent**: Use same acknowledgment style throughout document
5. **Be Compliant**: Follow your institution/venue guidelines first
6. **Be Responsible**: Always verify AI-generated content

## üéØ Bottom Line

**Core Message:** AI assistance is legitimate, productive, and increasingly common. Proper acknowledgment strengthens your work rather than weakening it.

**What Matters:**
- Transparency about tools used
- Clarity about human decision-making
- Responsibility for all content
- Following field-specific guidelines

**What Doesn't Matter:**
- Whether you used AI (many do)
- How much AI assistance you had
- Which AI tools you prefer

**What's Required:**
- Honest disclosure
- Human verification
- Taking responsibility
- No AI authorship claims
