**Q: Will this actually work?**  
A: Yes. Provisional patent applications are recognized legal instruments for establishing prior art. Patent examiners are required to consider them.

**Q: What if someone already filed a patent on this?**  
A: If they filed after this date, your filing defeats their patent. If they filed before, both exist as prior art, making it even harder for them to enforce.

A: No. Provisional patent applications can be filed pro se (without attorney). The USPTO process is designed for self-filing.

**Q: Can multiple people file the same manifest?**  
A: Yes! Multiple independent filings actually strengthen the prior art. Each person who files creates another official government record.
