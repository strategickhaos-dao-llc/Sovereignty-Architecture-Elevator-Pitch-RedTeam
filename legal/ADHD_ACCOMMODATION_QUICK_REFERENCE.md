## üéØ The 5-Minute Accommodation Request Template

### Subject Line:
```
Formal Request for ADHD Accommodations - [Your Name] - [Course/Program]
```

### Email Body Structure:

**1. DISCLOSURE (2 sentences)**
```
I am a student with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), 
a condition that substantially limits major life activities including 
concentration, task initiation, and time management. I am formally 
requesting reasonable accommodations under Section 504 of the 
Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. ¬ß 794) and the Americans with Disabilities 
Act.
```

**2. DOCUMENTATION (bullet list)**
```
I am providing the following documentation:
‚Ä¢ ADHD diagnosis from [provider/date]
‚Ä¢ Current medication prescription (Vyvanse/other)
‚Ä¢ Academic performance record (GPA: X.XX, Dean's List [if applicable])
‚Ä¢ Degree progress documentation (Degree Works: XX% complete)
‚Ä¢ [Any prior accommodation records]
```

**3. SPECIFIC ACCOMMODATIONS REQUESTED**
```
I respectfully request the following reasonable accommodations:
‚Ä¢ Extended deadlines for [specific assignments/modules]
‚Ä¢ Reopening of [Module X] for completion
‚Ä¢ Reduced late penalties for documented ADHD-related delays
‚Ä¢ [Acceptance of AI-assisted work with proper disclosure]
‚Ä¢ Academic adjustments per 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.44
```

**4. LEGAL CONTEXT (1-2 sentences)**
```
These accommodations are supported by precedent including Southeastern 
Community College v. Davis (442 U.S. 397), Guckenberger v. Boston University, 
and recent OCR Resolution Agreement: [University name if applicable]. 
Extended deadlines and module reopening constitute minor adjustments, 
not fundamental alterations to program requirements.
```

**5. INTERACTIVE PROCESS REQUEST**
```
I am willing to engage in an interactive process to determine appropriate 
accommodations. I request a written response within [10 business days] 
outlining approved accommodations or, if denied, the specific reasons why 
the requested accommodations would fundamentally alter program requirements.
```

**6. CONTACT INFO**
```
Thank you for your attention to this matter.

[Your Name]
[Student ID]
[Email]
[Phone - optional]
```

**7. ATTACHMENTS**
```
- ADHD_diagnosis.pdf
- prescription_vyvanse.pdf
- transcript_deanslist.pdf
- degree_progress.pdf
- [AI_tool_disclosure.pdf - if applicable]
```

## üìä Red Flags That Indicate Violation

üö© **University refuses to consider your documentation**  
‚Üí Violation: Failure to engage in interactive process

üö© **University demands "proof of bad intent" before accommodating**  
‚Üí Violation: AJT v. Osseo (2025) eliminated intent requirement

üö© **University threatens academic dishonesty charges for disclosed AI use**  
‚Üí Violation: Anti-retaliation (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12203)

üö© **University says "too late" for retroactive accommodations**  
‚Üí Violation: OCR consistently requires retroactive grade adjustments

üö© **University says accommodations would be "unfair to other students"**  
‚Üí Violation: ADA requires individualized assessment, not comparison to non-disabled

üö© **University ignores accommodation request entirely**  
‚Üí Violation: Nathanson v. Medical College (duty to investigate)

## ‚ö° Escalation Path

### Step 1: Formal Written Request
Use template above, send via email with read receipt

### Step 2: Follow Up (7-10 days)
If no response, send follow-up referencing 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.35 evaluation rights

### Step 3: Internal Appeal (if denied)
Reference specific legal authorities from [ADHD_ACCOMMODATION_LEGAL_FRAMEWORK.md](./ADHD_ACCOMMODATION_LEGAL_FRAMEWORK.md)

### Step 4: OCR Complaint (if still unresolved)
**File at**: Office for Civil Rights, U.S. Department of Education  
**Timeline**: Within 180 days of alleged discrimination  
**Include**: All correspondence, documentation, timeline of events  
**Remedies**: Grade changes, policy revisions, financial settlements

### Step 5: Legal Counsel (for complex cases)
Disability rights attorneys often work on contingency for strong cases

## üí° Pro Tips

### Documentation Best Practices
‚úÖ **Save everything**: All emails, letters, forms  
‚úÖ **Use certified mail**: For critical requests (creates proof of receipt)  
‚úÖ **Request written responses**: "Please respond in writing"  
‚úÖ **Note phone calls**: Date, time, person, summary - follow up with email confirmation  
‚úÖ **Organize chronologically**: Timeline matters for OCR complaints

### Communication Strategies
‚úÖ **Stay professional**: Emotion-free, fact-based language  
‚úÖ **Cite law frequently**: Shows you know your rights  
‚úÖ **Request specific**: "Reopen Module 2" not "help with grades"  
‚úÖ **Set deadlines**: "Please respond by [date]"  
‚úÖ **Copy appropriate offices**: Disability Services, Dean of Students, Registrar

### What NOT to Do
‚ùå Don't accept "policy prohibits" without question (policies can violate law)  
‚ùå Don't agree to withdraw instead of accommodations (your right to accommodations)  
‚ùå Don't let them rush you into decisions  
‚ùå Don't sign away your right to file complaints  
‚ùå Don't accept "we've never done this before" as a reason (law requires it)

## üéì Specific Scenarios

### Scenario: Failed Course Due to Late Work
**Request**: Grade recalculation with ADHD-related late penalties waived  
**Cite**: Maples v. UT Medical Branch (retakes not fundamental alteration)  
**Precedent**: OCR v. Capella (retroactive penalty waivers required)

### Scenario: Module Closed, Need Reopening
**Request**: Technical reopening of module for completion  
**Cite**: Push v. Western Michigan (ordered module reopening)  
**Precedent**: OCR v. Quinnipiac (must consider late work with documentation)

### Scenario: AI Tool Flagged as Plagiarism
**Request**: Review under accommodation framework, not academic dishonesty  
**Cite**: DOE AI Report 2023 (AI as accommodation), OMB M-24-10  
**Defense**: Disclosed use + citation = accommodation, not misconduct

### Scenario: "Too Much Accommodation Requested"
**Response**: ADA requires individualized assessment  
**Cite**: Davis (only denial = "fundamental alteration" shown)  
**Counter**: University must explain how each accommodation alters program

### Scenario: Retaliation After Request
**Action**: Document retaliation, file OCR complaint immediately  
**Cite**: 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12203, 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.61  
**Precedent**: OCR v. Purdue Global (retaliation prohibited)
