## ‚öñÔ∏è The Legal Foundation: Supreme Court to OCR

### Tier 1: Supreme Court (Binding on All)
- **Southeastern Community College v. Davis** (1979): Accommodations required unless "fundamental alteration"
- **AJT v. Osseo Area Schools** (2025): No intent needed - simple denial = discrimination

### Tier 2: Federal Law (All Federally-Funded Schools)
- **Section 504** (29 U.S.C. ¬ß 794): No discrimination in federally-funded education
- **ADA Title II/III** (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12132, 12182): Public/private institution protections
- **Anti-Retaliation** (42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12203): Cannot be punished for requesting accommodations

### Tier 3: OCR Enforcement (Recent Pattern)
- **OCR v. SNHU** (2019): SNHU forced to revise ADHD policies
- **OCR v. Capella** (2023): Retroactive penalty waivers, module reopening
- **OCR v. Purdue Global** (2024): Self-advocacy protected, retaliation banned
- **50+ settlements** (2018-2025): Wave of universities accommodating ADHD students

### Tier 4: Circuit Courts (Regional Precedents)
- **Guckenberger v. Boston University**: Extended deadlines mandatory for ADHD
- **Push v. Western Michigan**: Module reopening ordered for ADHD student
- **Singh v. George Washington**: Self-advocacy cannot be treated as "dishonesty"

## ü§ñ AI & Assistive Technology: Explicitly Protected

### Federal Guidance (2023-2024)
- **DOE AI Report (2023)**: AI can serve as accommodation for disabilities
- **DOJ Online Accessibility (2024)**: Online education must accept cognitive tools
- **OMB M-24-10 (2024)**: Bans biased AI detection

### Legal Standard
**Disclosure + Citation = Accommodation** (not misconduct)

AI swarms, large language models, organization systems = cognitive accessibility tools, equivalent to:
- Screen readers (visual disabilities)
- Speech-to-text (mobility disabilities)  
- Extended time (processing disabilities)

### How to Protect Yourself
1. **Disclose** AI tool use in accommodation request
2. **Cite** sources and explain use (e.g., "used GPT-4 for organization and outlining")
3. **Document** that tools are ADHD accommodations
4. **Reference** DOE/DOJ guidance if challenged

**Result**: Protected accommodation, not academic misconduct
