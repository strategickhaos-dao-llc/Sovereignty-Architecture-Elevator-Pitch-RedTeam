## ARTICLE I: ASSIGNMENT OF ROYALTY INTEREST

### 1.1 Grant of Irrevocable Royalty Interest
Assignor hereby **irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys** to Assignee an undivided **seven percent (7%)** interest in all Gross Royalty Income (as defined below) derived from the IP Portfolio, net only of Documented Platform Fees (as defined below).

This assignment is:
- **Irrevocable** - Cannot be revoked, terminated, or modified by Assignor
- **Perpetual** - Continues until the earlier of: (i) dissolution of Assignor, or (ii) complete liquidation of the IP Portfolio
- **Comprehensive** - Applies to all present and future IP owned or controlled by Assignor
- **Senior** - Takes priority over distributions to Members except as required by law

### 1.2 Definitions

#### (a) Gross Royalty Income
"Gross Royalty Income" means all revenue received by Assignor from:
- Software licenses (commercial, enterprise, and subscription)
- SaaS platform fees and usage charges
- API access fees and consumption-based billing
- Marketplace commissions (GitHub Marketplace, app stores, etc.)
- Content licensing and syndication
- AI model licensing and inference fees
- Consulting and implementation services using the IP Portfolio
- Equity or tokens received as payment (valued at fair market value)
- Any other revenue directly attributable to the IP Portfolio

#### (b) Documented Platform Fees
"Documented Platform Fees" means fees charged by third-party platforms for distribution, payment processing, or infrastructure, including:
- App store commissions (Apple App Store, Google Play, etc.)
- Marketplace fees (GitHub Marketplace, AWS Marketplace, etc.)
- Payment processor fees (Stripe, PayPal, etc.) up to 3%
- Infrastructure costs directly attributable to delivery (CDN, hosting) up to 5%

**Excluded from Documented Platform Fees:**
- Internal operational expenses (salaries, office, general overhead)
- Marketing and sales expenses
- Research and development costs
- Legal and accounting fees

#### (c) Net Royalty Receipts
"Net Royalty Receipts" means:
```
Gross Royalty Income
- Documented Platform Fees
= Net Royalty Receipts
```

#### (d) Charitable Share
"Charitable Share" means:
```
Net Royalty Receipts × 7%
= Charitable Share (amount owed to Charity)
```

### 1.3 Scope of IP Portfolio
The IP Portfolio includes all intellectual property described in Schedule A, including but not limited to:

- StrategicKhaos Sovereignty Architecture and all components
- PsycheVille platforms and content
- TRS (The Refinory System) AI infrastructure
- Valoryield Engine economic systems
- All associated software repositories, documentation, and content
- All derivative works and future developments

The IP Portfolio inventory in Schedule A shall be updated annually and provided to Charity.

## ARTICLE III: CHARITY RIGHTS AND PROTECTIONS

### 3.1 Audit Rights
Charity shall have the right to:

(a) **Annual Audit**: Once per calendar year, upon 30 days' written notice, audit Assignor's books and records related to:
- Royalty revenue recognition and collection
- Platform fee deductions
- Payment calculations

(b) **Audit Scope**: Audits may include:
- Review of financial statements and general ledger
- Examination of contracts and invoices
- Verification of payment processor reports
- Analysis of deduction methodologies

(c) **Audit Costs**: 
- Charity bears its own audit costs for routine annual audits
- If audit reveals underpayment exceeding 10%, Assignor shall reimburse reasonable audit costs

(d) **Access**: Assignor shall provide auditors with:
- On-site or virtual access to financial systems
- Electronic copies of requested documents
- Reasonable cooperation from financial personnel

### 3.2 Late Payment Interest
If any Charitable Share payment is not made within the required time frame:
- Interest shall accrue at **8% per annum** (or the maximum legal rate, whichever is less)
- Interest accrues from the due date until payment is received
- Late payments do not cure the default and may trigger other remedies

### 3.3 Enforcement Rights
In the event of non-payment or material breach, Charity may:
- Demand immediate payment of all unpaid amounts
- Exercise audit rights at any time (not limited to annual audits)
- Seek injunctive relief to compel compliance
- Recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs

### 3.4 Priority of Payment
The 7% Charitable Share shall have priority over:
- Distributions to Members or equity holders
- Bonuses and discretionary payments
- Non-essential operational expenses
- Debt repayment to related parties

Exception: Payments required by law (taxes, statutory obligations, bona fide third-party secured creditors) take precedence.

### 5.1 Charitable Contribution Intent
The parties intend this assignment to qualify as a charitable contribution under I.R.C. § 170, specifically:
- A present interest in income-producing property under I.R.C. § 170(f)(3)(A)
- Subject to qualified appraisal requirements if value exceeds $5,000
- Eligible for income tax deduction in the year of transfer

### 5.2 IRS Forms and Documentation
**Assignor's Responsibilities:**
- File Form 8283 (Noncash Charitable Contributions) with tax return if deduction claimed
- Obtain qualified appraisal if required (property value > $5,000)
- Provide Charity with completed Form 8283, Section B for signature

**Charity's Responsibilities:**
- Issue annual acknowledgment letters under I.R.C. § 170(f)(8)
- Sign Form 8283, Section B if requested (Donee Acknowledgment)
- File Form 8899 (Notice of Income from Donated Intellectual Property) if required
- Maintain records for IRS audit defense

### 5.3 Form 8899 Reporting
If Assignor claims a charitable deduction exceeding $5,000, and Charity receives income from the IP Portfolio:
- Charity shall file Form 8899 annually for the first 10 years
- Charity shall report net income received each year
- Assignor shall provide information necessary for Form 8899

### 5.4 Appraisal
If qualified appraisal is required:
- Assignor shall engage qualified appraiser at Assignor's expense
- Appraiser must meet IRS requirements (Treas. Reg. § 1.170A-13)
- Appraisal must be dated within 60 days of contribution
- Copy provided to Charity for records

### 5.5 Substantiation
Charity's annual acknowledgment letters shall state:
- Amount of royalty payments received in the calendar year
- No goods or services provided in exchange (or description if any)
- Confirmation of Charity's 501(c)(3) status

## ARTICLE VII: TERM, AMENDMENT, AND TERMINATION

### 7.1 Irrevocability
This Agreement is **IRREVOCABLE** and may not be terminated by Assignor under any circumstances except as provided in Section 7.4.

### 7.2 Perpetual Term
This Agreement continues in perpetuity until the earlier of:
- Complete liquidation of the IP Portfolio (no remaining IP or revenue potential)
- Dissolution and wind-up of Assignor under Wyoming law
- Mutual written agreement of both parties (requires extraordinary circumstances)

### 7.3 Amendment
This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties. Amendments may:
- Increase (but never decrease) the percentage above 7%
- Update administrative details (payment methods, contact information)
- Clarify definitions without changing economic substance

Amendments that decrease Charity's rights are void.

### 7.4 Termination Events
This Agreement automatically terminates only if:

(a) **Charity Loss of Status**: Charity loses 501(c)(3) status and no successor charity is designated within 90 days

(b) **IP Portfolio Exhaustion**: IP Portfolio is completely liquidated with no remaining assets or revenue potential, certified by independent auditor

(c) **Assignor Dissolution**: Assignor is dissolved and all assets are liquidated under Wyoming law

### 7.5 Successor Beneficiary
If Charity loses 501(c)(3) status, dissolves, or is unable to receive payments:
- Assignor shall designate a successor 501(c)(3) organization with a similar charitable purpose
- Designation requires approval of Assignor's Members
- During transition (up to 90 days), payments held in escrow
- Charity's audit rights and remedies continue during transition
