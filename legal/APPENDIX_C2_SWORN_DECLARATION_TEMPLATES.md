## Important Legal Notes

### What is a Sworn Declaration?

A sworn declaration is a written statement made under penalty of perjury. In U.S. federal courts and most state courts, declarations under 28 U.S.C. § 1746 have the same legal effect as notarized affidavits but don't require a notary.

**Key Requirements:**
- Must be based on personal knowledge
- Must be truthful (making false statements is perjury)
- Must include specific declaration language
- Must be signed and dated

### When to Use These Templates

**Appropriate Uses:**
- ✅ Court filings (with attorney guidance)
- ✅ Patent/IP applications and disputes
- ✅ Investor due diligence packages
- ✅ Academic dissertation appendices
- ✅ Business contract disputes
- ✅ Regulatory compliance documentation

**Not Needed For:**
- ❌ Informal documentation
- ❌ Internal team use
- ❌ Casual investor conversations
- ❌ Personal record-keeping

### Attorney Review Required

**⚠️ CRITICAL:** These templates provide general forms. Before using any sworn declaration in:
- Court proceedings
- Government filings
- Disputes involving significant stakes

**You must consult with an attorney licensed in the relevant jurisdiction.** The attorney should review and customize the declaration for your specific situation.

## Template 1: General R&D Audit Trail Authentication

**Use Case:** Authenticating your AI conversation ledger as a business record for R&D purposes

**Jurisdiction:** U.S. Federal Courts (28 U.S.C. § 1746)

---

### DECLARATION OF DOMENIC GARZA

I, Domenic Garza, declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the United States that the following is true and correct:

1. **Personal Knowledge:** I make this declaration based on my personal knowledge, and if called as a witness, I could and would testify competently to the matters stated herein.

2. **Identity and Role:** My name is Domenic Garza. I am the founder and principal operator of Strategickhaos DAO LLC, a cybersecurity research and private investigation firm. I am personally responsible for maintaining the firm's research and development records.

3. **AI Conversation Ledger System:** Since [START DATE], I have maintained a digital ledger system (the "AI Conversation Ledger") documenting my interactions with artificial intelligence language models as part of my R&D work. This ledger is kept in the regular course of my business activities.

4. **Ledger Purpose:** The AI Conversation Ledger serves as my digital research notebook, documenting:
   - Research questions posed to AI models
   - Responses and analyses received
   - Cross-validation across multiple AI systems
   - Decision-making processes and rationale
   - Technical architecture development
   - Due diligence and risk analysis

5. **Ledger Maintenance Process:** I maintain the AI Conversation Ledger according to a systematic process:
   
   a. **Contemporaneous Entry:** I create ledger entries at or near the time of each significant AI conversation, typically within [TIMEFRAME, e.g., "24 hours" or "the same day"].
   
   b. **Content Recording:** Each entry includes:
      - Timestamp of the conversation
      - AI platform and model used (e.g., "Claude 3 Opus", "GPT-4")
      - URL link to the original conversation (share URL)
      - Summary of topics discussed
      - Key insights or decisions made
      - My verification that the entry accurately reflects the conversation
   
   c. **Cryptographic Verification:** Each entry includes:
      - SHA-3-256 hash of the entry content
      - Hash of the previous entry (creating a tamper-evident chain)
      - My GPG digital signature
      - Git commit with signed commit signature
   
   d. **Quality Control:** I personally review each entry for accuracy before finalizing it.

6. **Regular Business Practice:** Maintaining this ledger is part of my regular business practice. I rely on these records to:
   - Track the development of research ideas
   - Document due diligence in decision-making
   - Maintain an audit trail for potential IP claims
   - Provide transparency to investors and collaborators
   - Ensure methodological rigor in my work

7. **Authenticity of Attached Records:** Attached as Exhibit A is a true and accurate copy of [DESCRIBE SPECIFIC RECORDS, e.g., "AI Conversation Ledger entries from November 1-21, 2025"]. These records were created and maintained by me in the regular course of business at or near the time of the events recorded.

8. **Verification Method:** Anyone can verify the authenticity and integrity of these records by:
   - Checking the GPG signatures against my public key
   - Verifying the git commit signatures in the public repository
   - Accessing the original AI conversations via the share URLs
   - Validating the cryptographic hash chain
   - [OPTIONAL: Verifying blockchain timestamps via OpenTimestamps]

9. **No Alterations:** The attached records have not been altered, modified, or tampered with since their creation. The cryptographic signatures and hash chain provide independent verification of this fact.

10. **Personal Verification:** I have personally verified that each share URL in the attached records links to the actual conversation I had with the respective AI system, and that the ledger entry accurately reflects the content of that conversation.

I declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct.

## Instructions for Use

### Step 1: Choose the Right Template

Select the template that matches your use case:
- **Template 1:** General business records authentication
- **Template 2:** Patent or IP disputes
- **Template 3:** Academic work documentation
- **Template 4:** Investor presentations
- **Template 5:** Trade secret protection

### Step 2: Customize the Template

1. Replace all bracketed [PLACEHOLDERS] with your specific information
2. Remove any irrelevant sections
3. Add any additional relevant facts
4. Ensure all statements are true and based on your personal knowledge

### Step 3: Attorney Review

**Before signing:**
- Consult with an attorney licensed in the relevant jurisdiction
- Have the attorney review and approve the customized declaration
- Discuss any jurisdiction-specific requirements
- Understand the legal implications of signing under penalty of perjury

### Step 4: Prepare Exhibits

1. **Exhibit A:** Attach relevant ledger entries
   - Use clean, professional formatting
   - Include all verification information (GPG signatures, hashes, etc.)
   - Ensure share URLs are accessible (or note if private)
   - Include instructions for technical verification

2. **Additional Exhibits:**
   - Your public GPG key
   - Git repository commit history (if relevant)
   - Blockchain timestamp certificates (if applicable)
   - Screenshots of share URLs (if needed)

### Step 5: Execute the Declaration

1. **Fill in the date and location**
2. **Sign in ink** (blue ink is preferred to show original)
3. **Keep the original** for your records
4. **Provide copies** as needed to courts, investors, etc.

### Step 6: Maintain Records

- Keep the original signed declaration in a secure location
- Maintain copies of all exhibits referenced
- Preserve the electronic evidence (ledger, git repo, etc.) in its original state
- Document any subsequent changes in a new declaration if needed

## International Adaptations

### For Other U.S. State Courts

Most states have adopted equivalent declaration statutes. Check your state's rules and modify the language:

**Example State Law Declaration Language:**
```
"I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of the State of [STATE] 
that the foregoing is true and correct."
```

### For Non-U.S. Jurisdictions

#### United Kingdom
Replace U.S. declaration language with:
```
STATEMENT OF TRUTH

I believe that the facts stated in this declaration are true. 
I understand that proceedings for contempt of court may be brought 
against anyone who makes, or causes to be made, a false statement 
in a document verified by a statement of truth without an honest 
belief in its truth.

Signed: _________________ 
Dated: _________________
```

#### European Union
Consider formal notarization or qualified electronic signature:
- Notarize before a notary public or equivalent
- Use eIDAS-qualified electronic signature service
- Follow national civil procedure rules for declarations/affidavits

#### Canada
Use "Affidavit" format with notary jurat:
```
SWORN (or AFFIRMED) before me at the [City/Municipality] 
of _____________, in the Province of _____________, 
this _____ day of _____________, 20____.

____________________           ____________________
A Commissioner for Oaths      Signature of Deponent
```

#### Australia
Use "Affidavit" or "Statutory Declaration" format:
```
This statutory declaration is made under the Statutory Declarations Act 1959.
I declare that the contents of this statement are true.

Declared at _____________ in the State of _____________
on _____ day of _____________, 20____.

Before me: ____________________
[Signature of authorized witness]
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Don't Do This:

1. **Making statements beyond your personal knowledge**
   - ❌ "Industry experts agree that..."
   - ✅ "Based on my research, I concluded that..."

2. **Including legal conclusions**
   - ❌ "This proves I own the patent rights"
   - ✅ "I conceived and developed this invention as documented"

3. **Exaggerating or overstating**
   - ❌ "This is the best/only solution"
   - ✅ "I developed this approach because..."

4. **Making absolute statements you can't verify**
   - ❌ "No one else has ever done this"
   - ✅ "To the best of my knowledge after conducting research..."

5. **Backdating or altering records**
   - ❌ Creating "contemporaneous" records after the fact
   - ✅ Honestly stating when records were created

### ✅ Do This:

1. **Stick to your personal knowledge**
2. **Be specific and factual**
3. **Qualify statements appropriately** ("to the best of my knowledge", "based on my research")
4. **Admit limitations** where appropriate
5. **Be completely truthful** - perjury is a serious crime

## Legal Effect and Consequences

### What Happens When You Sign

**You are making legally binding statements:**
- False statements constitute perjury (felony)
- Courts can impose sanctions for false declarations
- Your credibility can be challenged in cross-examination
- Opposing parties may investigate your claims

**Penalties for Perjury:**
- Federal: Up to 5 years imprisonment (18 U.S.C. § 1621)
- State: Varies, typically 1-10 years imprisonment
- Professional consequences (license revocation, etc.)
- Civil liability for damages caused

### When to Reconsider

**Don't sign if:**
- You're not completely certain of the facts
- You're making statements beyond your knowledge
- You're being pressured without attorney review
- You don't fully understand what you're signing
- The stakes are high and you haven't consulted an attorney
