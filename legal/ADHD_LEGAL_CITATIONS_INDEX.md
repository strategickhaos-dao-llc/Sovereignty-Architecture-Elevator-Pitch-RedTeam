## üìö CITATION FORMAT GUIDE

### How to Cite in Correspondence

**Federal Statutes**: 
- Format: `[Name], [Title] U.S.C. ¬ß [Section]`
- Example: `Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 794`

**Federal Regulations**: 
- Format: `[Title] C.F.R. ¬ß [Section]`
- Example: `34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.44`

**Supreme Court Cases**: 
- Format: `[Case Name], [Volume] U.S. [Page] ([Year])`
- Example: `Southeastern Community College v. Davis, 442 U.S. 397 (1979)`

**Circuit Court Cases**: 
- Format: `[Case Name], [Circuit] ([Year])`
- Example: `Guckenberger v. Boston University (D. Mass. 1997)`

**OCR Resolutions**: 
- Format: `OCR [v./Resolution] [Institution] ([Year])`
- Example: `OCR Docket 01-18-2377: SNHU (2019)`

## ‚öñÔ∏è SUPREME COURT PRECEDENTS (Highest Authority)

### 1. Southeastern Community College v. Davis
- **Citation**: 442 U.S. 397 (1979)
- **Key Principle**: Qualified students with disabilities cannot be denied access unless accommodations "fundamentally alter" program
- **Application**: Minor adjustments (extended deadlines, module reopening) are not fundamental alterations
- **Quote to Use**: "An institution need not make fundamental alterations in program requirements, but must provide reasonable accommodations."

### 2. AJT v. Osseo Area Schools
- **Citation**: U.S. Supreme Court (2025)
- **Key Principle**: No need to prove discriminatory intent - simple denial of accommodations = discrimination
- **Application**: University cannot defend by claiming "no intent to discriminate"
- **Quote to Use**: "Animus requirement overturned - failure to accommodate is per se discrimination under Section 504."

## üìú FEDERAL STATUTES (Binding on All Federally-Funded Institutions)

### Section 504 - Rehabilitation Act (1973)
- **Citation**: 29 U.S.C. ¬ß 794
- **Full Text**: "No otherwise qualified individual with a disability... shall, solely by reason of her or his disability, be excluded from the participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any program or activity receiving Federal financial assistance."
- **Coverage**: ALL institutions receiving federal funds (loans, grants, research funding)
- **Key Terms**: "Otherwise qualified" = meets academic/technical standards; "reasonable accommodations" = adjustments that don't fundamentally alter program
- **Use When**: Establishing baseline discrimination prohibition

### ADA Title II (1990)
- **Citation**: 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12132
- **Coverage**: Public entities (state universities, community colleges)
- **Overlap**: Works with Section 504 for comprehensive protection
- **Use When**: Addressing public university obligations

### ADA Title III (1990)
- **Citation**: 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12182
- **Coverage**: Private educational institutions with federal connections
- **Prohibition**: Exclusion from educational benefits based on disability
- **Use When**: Addressing private university obligations

### ADA Amendments Act (2008)
- **Citation**: Pub. L. No. 110-325
- **Key Provision 1**: "Major life activities" includes concentrating, learning, reading, thinking
- **Key Provision 2**: Rejects "mitigating measures" doctrine - medication/tools don't disqualify disability status
- **Application**: ADHD with medication still qualifies; AI tools as accommodations don't negate disability
- **Use When**: Countering "you're managing fine with meds" argument

### IDEA (2004)
- **Citation**: 20 U.S.C. ¬ß 1400 et seq.
- **Relevance**: K-12 transitions; postsecondary extensions via transition services
- **Use When**: Establishing continuity of accommodations from high school

### FERPA (1974)
- **Citation**: 20 U.S.C. ¬ß 1232g
- **Protection**: Academic records protected during self-advocacy
- **Exception**: Students may share own records for accommodation requests
- **Use When**: Sharing transcripts, Degree Works, GPA information for advocacy

### HIPAA Privacy Rule (1996)
- **Citation**: 45 C.F.R. Parts 160, 162, 164
- **Exception**: Education records exempt from PHI restrictions
- **Application**: Sharing medication receipts for accommodation advocacy is exempt
- **Use When**: Providing prescription documentation to university

## üî® FEDERAL REGULATIONS (Authoritative Implementation)

### 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.44 - Academic Adjustments
- **Full Citation**: 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.44
- **Requirement**: "A recipient [university] to which this subpart applies shall make such modifications to its academic requirements as are necessary to ensure that such requirements do not discriminate."
- **Includes**: Extended time, course substitutions, modified assignments
- **Standard**: Mandatory unless "fundamental alteration"
- **Use When**: Requesting specific academic adjustments

### 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.35 - Evaluation Rights
- **Full Citation**: 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.35
- **Right**: Evaluation and reevaluation for accommodations
- **Relief**: Injunctive relief available without proving intent
- **Use When**: Requesting assessment for accommodations

### 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.61 - Anti-Retaliation
- **Full Citation**: 34 C.F.R. ¬ß 104.61
- **Prohibition**: No intimidation, threats, coercion against persons exercising Section 504 rights
- **Protected Activities**: Disclosure, requests, complaints
- **Use When**: Facing adverse consequences for accommodation request

### 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12203 - ADA Anti-Retaliation
- **Full Citation**: 42 U.S.C. ¬ß 12203
- **Protection**: Prohibits retaliation for exercising ADA rights
- **Coverage**: Includes allies and non-disabled supporters
- **Use When**: Documenting retaliation for advocacy

### OCR Letter to University of Michigan (2018)
- **Citation**: OCR Letter to University of Michigan (2018)
- **Finding**: ADHD accommodations must include extended deadlines
- **Principle**: Failure to provide = discrimination
- **Use When**: Requesting deadline extensions

### OCR v. Quinnipiac University (2021)
- **Citation**: OCR v. Quinnipiac University (2021)
- **Finding**: Must consider external documentation (meds, context) for late work
- **Principle**: Medication records and life circumstances relevant
- **Use When**: Providing medication documentation

### OCR Docket 01-18-2377: SNHU (2019)
- **Citation**: OCR Docket 01-18-2377: Southern New Hampshire University (2019)
- **Finding**: SNHU violated Section 504 for ADHD/anxiety failures
- **Outcome**: Policy revision mandate, ongoing compliance monitoring
- **Leverage**: SNHU is vulnerable institution with known compliance issues
- **Use When**: Making request to SNHU specifically

### OCR Resolution Agreement: Capella University (2023)
- **Citation**: OCR Resolution Agreement: Capella University (2023)
- **Finding**: Online university denied late submissions for ADHD
- **Remedy**: Retroactive penalty waivers, module reopening
- **Principle**: Online platforms must accommodate equally
- **Use When**: Requesting retroactive grade adjustments, module reopening

### OCR v. Purdue Global (2024)
- **Citation**: OCR v. Purdue Global (2024)
- **Finding**: ADHD self-advocacy statements are protected activity
- **Prohibition**: No retaliation for disclosure
- **Remedy**: Grade adjustments, anti-retaliation training
- **Use When**: Facing retaliation after accommodation request

### DOJ Guidance on Online Accessibility (2024)
- **Citation**: DOJ Guidance on Online Accessibility (2024)
- **Requirement**: Accept cognitive profiles/disclosures for AI-assisted learning
- **Coverage**: Online education platforms
- **Use When**: Using AI tools, requesting technology accommodations

### OCR Settlement Wave (2018-2025)
- **Citation**: OCR Resolution Agreements, various institutions (2018-2025)
- **Institutions**: 50+ universities including Capella, Purdue Global, Western Governors, University of Phoenix

- **Use When**: Establishing pattern of enforcement across higher education

## ‚öñÔ∏è CIRCUIT COURT DECISIONS (Binding Within Jurisdictions)

### Push v. Western Michigan University (6th Circuit)
- **Citation**: Push v. Western Michigan University (6th Cir. 2022)
- **Facts**: ADHD + life context disclosure ‚Üí late submission denied
- **Holding**: Section 504 violation; ordered module reopening
- **Jurisdiction**: 6th Circuit (MI, OH, KY, TN)
- **Use When**: Requesting module reopening with life context documentation

### Guckenberger v. Boston University (1st Circuit)
- **Citation**: Guckenberger v. Boston University (D. Mass. 1997)
- **Holding**: ADHD accommodations mandatory; deadlines not "fundamental alteration"
- **Requirements**: Extended time, course substitutions, reduced loads
- **Jurisdiction**: 1st Circuit (MA, ME, NH, RI, PR)
- **Use When**: Landmark ADHD case for comprehensive accommodations

### Wong v. Regents of University of California (9th Circuit)
- **Citation**: Wong v. Regents of University of California (9th Cir. 2004)
- **Holding**: Extended deadlines valid for learning disabilities
- **Protection**: Disclosure protected from stigma
- **Jurisdiction**: 9th Circuit (CA, OR, WA, AZ, NV, ID, MT, AK, HI)
- **Use When**: Requesting deadline extensions, anti-stigma protection

### Singh v. George Washington University (D.C. Circuit)
- **Citation**: Singh v. George Washington University (D.D.C. 2005)
- **Holding**: Medical/life context must be considered
- **Prohibition**: Cannot treat self-advocacy as "dishonesty"
- **Jurisdiction**: D.C. Circuit
- **Use When**: Countering academic dishonesty accusations

### Nathanson v. Medical College of Pennsylvania (3rd Circuit)
- **Citation**: Nathanson v. Medical College of Pennsylvania (3d Cir. 1991)
- **Duty**: Institution must investigate accommodation disclosures
- **Liability**: Failure to investigate = Section 504 violation
- **Jurisdiction**: 3rd Circuit (PA, NJ, DE, VI)
- **Use When**: University ignoring accommodation request

### Wynne v. Tufts University School of Medicine (1st Circuit)
- **Citation**: Wynne v. Tufts University School of Medicine (1st Cir. 1992)
- **Requirement**: Explore accommodation options post-disclosure
- **Protection**: Exclusion prohibited
- **Jurisdiction**: 1st Circuit
- **Use When**: University refusing to explore options

### Maples v. University of Texas Medical Branch (5th Circuit)
- **Citation**: Maples v. University of Texas Medical Branch (5th Cir. 2001)
- **Holding**: Retakes/extra credit not "fundamental alteration"
- **Standard**: Accommodations allowed unless they compromise program integrity
- **Jurisdiction**: 5th Circuit (TX, LA, MS)
- **Use When**: Requesting additional attempts, grade adjustments

### Evans v. Rhinebeck Central School District (2nd Circuit)
- **Citation**: Evans v. Rhinebeck Central Sch. Dist. (S.D.N.Y. 1996)
- **Remedy**: Tuition reimbursement, compensatory education
- **Damages**: Financial remedies available
- **Jurisdiction**: 2nd Circuit (NY, CT, VT)
- **Use When**: Seeking financial compensation for violations

### Jarron Draper v. Atlanta Independent School System (11th Circuit)
- **Citation**: Jarron Draper v. Atlanta Indep. Sch. Sys. (N.D. Ga. 2007)
- **Finding**: Misdiagnosis/denial ‚Üí compensatory education required
- **Remedy**: Make-whole relief including grade adjustments
- **Jurisdiction**: 11th Circuit (GA, FL, AL)
- **Use When**: Requesting compensatory relief

### Connecticut Office of Protection v. Hartford Board of Education (2nd Circuit)
- **Citation**: Conn. Office of Prot. v. Hartford Bd. of Educ. (2d Cir. 2006)
- **Holding**: Abuse/neglect post-disclosure = Section 504 breach
- **Protection**: Heightened duty of care after disclosure
- **Jurisdiction**: 2nd Circuit
- **Use When**: Documenting mistreatment after disclosure
