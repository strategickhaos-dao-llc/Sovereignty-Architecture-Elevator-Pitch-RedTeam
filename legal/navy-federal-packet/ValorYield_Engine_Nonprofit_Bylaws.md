## ARTICLE I: NAME AND PURPOSE

### Section 1.1 - Name
The name of this corporation is **ValorYield Engine** (the "Corporation").

### Section 1.2 - Principal Office
The principal office of the Corporation shall be located in the State of Wyoming, with the specific address to be designated by the Board of Directors.

### Section 1.3 - Registered Agent
The Corporation shall maintain a registered agent in the State of Wyoming as required by law.

### Section 1.4 - Purpose
The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), including:

a) **Education and Research in Artificial Intelligence:**
   - Conducting research in artificial intelligence safety, alignment, and ethics
   - Developing open-source AI tools and frameworks for public benefit
   - Providing education and training in AI technology and responsible AI development
   - Publishing research findings and educational materials

b) **Community Benefit Programs:**
   - Supporting workforce development in technology fields
   - Providing resources and training to underserved communities
   - Promoting digital literacy and access to technology
   - Supporting veterans and military families in technology careers

c) **Scientific Research:**
   - Advancing the field of quantum-symbolic computing
   - Researching AI alignment and interpretability
   - Developing frameworks for AI governance and ethics
   - Collaborating with academic and research institutions

d) **Public Education:**
   - Creating and distributing educational content on AI and technology
   - Hosting workshops, seminars, and conferences
   - Developing curriculum and training materials
   - Operating educational programs and initiatives

The Corporation shall not engage in any activities that are not permitted to be carried on by:
- A corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or
- A corporation, contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code

### Section 1.5 - Tax-Exempt Status
The Corporation is organized and shall be operated exclusively for the purposes set forth above, and no part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation shall be authorized to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of its exempt purposes.

## ARTICLE II: CORPORATE STRUCTURE

### Section 2.1 - Nonprofit Status
The Corporation is a nonprofit corporation organized under the Wyoming Nonprofit Corporation Act and shall be operated exclusively for the purposes described in Article I.

### Section 2.2 - Federal EIN
The Corporation's Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) is: 39-2923503

### Section 2.3 - 501(c)(3) Status
The Corporation has been organized to qualify for tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. All provisions of these Bylaws shall be interpreted consistent with maintaining such status.

### Section 2.4 - No Private Inurement
No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, members, or other private persons, except that the Corporation shall be authorized and empowered to:

a) Pay reasonable compensation for services rendered to the Corporation
b) Make payments and distributions in furtherance of the exempt purposes set forth in Article I
c) Make distributions to organizations that qualify as exempt organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code

## ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP

### Section 3.1 - No Membership
The Corporation shall have no members. All rights and powers that would otherwise vest in the members shall vest in the Board of Directors.

### Section 3.2 - Participants and Beneficiaries
The Corporation may have participants, beneficiaries, donors, and supporters who do not have membership rights but who may receive services, participate in programs, or support the Corporation's mission.

---

## ARTICLE IV: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 4.1 - General Powers
The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by its Board of Directors, which shall have and may exercise all the powers of the Corporation, subject to the limitations of the Wyoming Nonprofit Corporation Act, the Articles of Incorporation, and these Bylaws.

### Section 4.2 - Number and Qualifications
The Board of Directors shall consist of not fewer than three (3) and not more than fifteen (15) directors. The exact number shall be fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board of Directors.

**Initial Board of Directors:**
- Domenic G. Garza (Founder and President)
- [Director 2 - To be appointed]
- [Director 3 - To be appointed]

Directors shall be individuals committed to the Corporation's mission and purposes. Directors need not be residents of Wyoming.

### Section 4.3 - Term of Office
Directors shall serve for staggered terms of three (3) years, with approximately one-third of the Board elected each year. Directors may serve consecutive terms without limitation.

### Section 4.4 - Election of Directors
Directors shall be elected by the existing Board of Directors. Nominations may be made by any director, and election shall require the affirmative vote of a majority of the directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present.

### Section 4.5 - Resignation and Removal

**Resignation:** Any director may resign at any time by giving written notice to the President or Secretary. Such resignation shall take effect at the time specified in the notice or, if no time is specified, immediately upon receipt.

**Removal:** Any director may be removed, with or without cause, by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the entire Board of Directors at a meeting called for that purpose.

### Section 4.6 - Vacancies
Vacancies on the Board of Directors, including vacancies resulting from an increase in the number of directors, may be filled by a majority vote of the remaining directors. A director elected to fill a vacancy shall serve the unexpired term of his or her predecessor.

### Section 4.7 - Compensation
Directors shall serve without compensation. However, directors may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of their duties, including travel, lodging, and meals.

### Section 4.8 - Conflicts of Interest
The Board shall adopt and maintain a conflict of interest policy. Directors must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest and abstain from voting on matters in which they have a conflict.

---

## ARTICLE V: MEETINGS OF THE BOARD

### Section 5.1 - Annual Meeting
The Board of Directors shall hold an annual meeting for the purpose of electing officers, appointing committees, and transacting such other business as may properly come before the meeting. The annual meeting shall be held at such time and place as determined by the Board.

### Section 5.2 - Regular Meetings
Regular meetings of the Board of Directors may be held at such times and places as the Board may determine by resolution. Notice of regular meetings shall be provided at least ten (10) days in advance.

### Section 5.3 - Special Meetings
Special meetings of the Board of Directors may be called by the President or by any three (3) directors. Notice of special meetings shall be provided at least five (5) days in advance and shall state the purpose of the meeting.

### Section 5.4 - Notice of Meetings
Notice of meetings may be given by:
- Electronic mail
- Written notice delivered personally or by mail
- Telephone or text message
- Other electronic means

### Section 5.5 - Waiver of Notice
Any director may waive notice of any meeting before, during, or after the meeting. Attendance at a meeting shall constitute waiver of notice unless the director objects to the lack of proper notice at the beginning of the meeting.

### Section 5.6 - Quorum
A majority of the directors then in office shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. If a quorum is not present, the meeting may be adjourned to a later time without further notice.

### Section 5.7 - Voting
Each director shall have one vote. The act of a majority of the directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board of Directors, unless a greater vote is required by law, the Articles of Incorporation, or these Bylaws.

### Section 5.8 - Participation by Electronic Means
Directors may participate in meetings by conference telephone, video conference, or similar communications equipment that allows all persons participating to hear each other. Participation in such manner shall constitute presence in person at the meeting.

### Section 5.9 - Action Without a Meeting
Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of the Board of Directors may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing to such action. Such written consent shall have the same force and effect as a unanimous vote.

## ARTICLE VI: OFFICERS

### Section 6.1 - Officers
The officers of the Corporation shall be:
- President
- Vice President (optional)
- Secretary
- Treasurer

The Board of Directors may create additional officer positions as needed.

### Section 6.2 - Election and Term
Officers shall be elected annually by the Board of Directors at the annual meeting. Officers shall serve one-year terms and may be re-elected without limitation.

### Section 6.3 - Removal
Any officer may be removed by the Board of Directors with or without cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the entire Board.

### Section 6.4 - Vacancies
A vacancy in any office may be filled by the Board of Directors for the unexpired portion of the term.

### Section 6.5 - President
The President shall be the chief executive officer of the Corporation and shall:
- Preside at all meetings of the Board of Directors
- Have general supervision over the business and affairs of the Corporation
- Execute documents on behalf of the Corporation
- Perform such other duties as assigned by the Board

### Section 6.6 - Vice President
The Vice President (if elected) shall:
- Perform the duties of the President in the President's absence
- Assist the President in the management of the Corporation
- Perform such other duties as assigned by the President or Board

### Section 6.7 - Secretary
The Secretary shall:
- Keep accurate minutes of all Board meetings
- Maintain corporate records and documents
- Provide notices of meetings as required
- Authenticate records of the Corporation
- Perform such other duties as assigned by the President or Board

### Section 6.8 - Treasurer
The Treasurer shall:
- Have custody of all corporate funds and securities
- Keep accurate financial records
- Prepare financial reports for the Board
- Ensure proper filing of tax returns and financial reports
- Oversee banking relationships and financial accounts
- Perform such other duties as assigned by the President or Board

---

## ARTICLE VII: COMMITTEES

### Section 7.1 - Standing Committees
The Board of Directors may establish standing committees as needed, which may include:
- Finance Committee
- Fundraising Committee
- Program Committee
- Governance Committee
- Audit Committee (required if annual revenues exceed certain thresholds)

### Section 7.2 - Special Committees
The Board may create special or ad hoc committees for specific purposes and limited durations.

### Section 7.3 - Committee Composition
Committees shall consist of at least two (2) directors appointed by the Board. Committees may include non-director advisors, but only directors may vote on committee matters.

### Section 7.4 - Committee Powers
Committees shall have such powers as delegated by the Board, except that no committee may:
- Amend the Articles of Incorporation or Bylaws
- Elect or remove directors or officers
- Adopt a plan of merger or dissolution
- Authorize distributions
- Approve transactions involving conflicts of interest

### Section 7.5 - Committee Meetings
Each committee shall meet as necessary and shall keep minutes of its meetings. Committee actions may be taken by electronic vote or written consent.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: BANKING AND FINANCIAL MATTERS

### Section 8.1 - Banking Relationships
The Corporation is authorized to establish and maintain banking relationships with financial institutions, including Navy Federal Credit Union, as determined by the Board of Directors or officers acting under delegated authority.

### Section 8.2 - Navy Federal Credit Union Accounts
The Corporation, through its President or Treasurer, is specifically authorized to:

a) Apply for and maintain business accounts with Navy Federal Credit Union, including:
   - Nonprofit checking accounts
   - Nonprofit savings accounts
   - Money market accounts
   - Other deposit accounts

b) Apply for nonprofit credit cards and other financial products as available and appropriate

c) Execute banking resolutions and related documents required by Navy Federal Credit Union

d) Designate authorized signatories for accounts

### Section 8.3 - Authorized Signatories
The President and Treasurer shall be authorized signatories on all bank accounts. The Board may designate additional authorized signatories as needed.

Checks, drafts, or other orders for payment in excess of $[Amount to be specified] shall require two (2) signatures or specific Board authorization.

### Section 8.4 - Fiscal Year
The fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year ending December 31, unless otherwise determined by the Board of Directors.

### Section 8.5 - Financial Records
The Corporation shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of account in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles applicable to nonprofit organizations.

### Section 8.6 - Annual Audit or Review
If required by law, regulation, or as determined by the Board, the Corporation shall have an annual audit or financial review conducted by an independent certified public accountant.

### Section 8.7 - Budget
The Treasurer, in consultation with the Finance Committee (if established), shall prepare an annual budget for Board approval. The budget shall be reviewed and updated as necessary throughout the year.

### Section 8.8 - Revenue and Funding Sources
The Corporation may receive revenue from:
- Charitable donations and contributions
- Grants from foundations, corporations, and government agencies
- Program service fees (subject to IRS regulations on unrelated business income)
- Investment income
- Fundraising events
- Contracted services consistent with exempt purposes

### Section 8.9 - Distribution Agreements
The Corporation may enter into agreements with related organizations (such as StrategicKhaos DAO LLC) for:
- Receipt of charitable contributions or program-related payments
- Provision of services consistent with exempt purposes
- Collaborative programs and initiatives

All such arrangements must:
- Serve the Corporation's exempt purposes
- Be at arm's length and fair market value
- Comply with IRS regulations regarding private benefit and inurement
- Be documented in written agreements approved by the Board

## ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION AND INSURANCE

### Section 9.1 - Indemnification
The Corporation shall indemnify directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by the Wyoming Nonprofit Corporation Act and applicable law, against expenses, judgments, fines, and settlements incurred in connection with any proceeding arising from their service to the Corporation, provided they acted in good faith and in the best interests of the Corporation.

### Section 9.2 - Insurance
The Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of directors, officers, employees, and agents against liability asserted against or incurred by them in their official capacities.

### Section 9.3 - Advancement of Expenses
The Corporation may advance expenses incurred in defending any proceeding, subject to an undertaking by the recipient to repay such amounts if it is ultimately determined that indemnification is not appropriate.

---

## ARTICLE X: GOVERNANCE POLICIES

### Section 10.1 - Conflict of Interest Policy
The Board shall adopt and maintain a conflict of interest policy requiring disclosure of financial interests and recusal from decisions involving conflicts.

### Section 10.2 - Whistleblower Policy
The Board shall adopt a whistleblower policy protecting individuals who report suspected violations of law or policy in good faith.

### Section 10.3 - Document Retention Policy
The Board shall adopt a document retention and destruction policy specifying retention periods for corporate records and documents.

### Section 10.4 - Investment Policy
The Board may adopt an investment policy governing the management and investment of corporate funds, consistent with maintaining tax-exempt status.

### Section 10.5 - Gift Acceptance Policy
The Board may adopt a gift acceptance policy specifying what types of gifts the Corporation will accept and under what conditions.

---

## ARTICLE XI: DISSOLUTION

### Section 11.1 - Dissolution Process
The Corporation may be dissolved by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the entire Board of Directors, followed by appropriate filings with the Wyoming Secretary of State and the IRS.

### Section 11.2 - Distribution of Assets
Upon dissolution of the Corporation, after paying or providing for all debts and obligations, all remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations:

a) Organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code

b) Which have established their tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3)

c) Selected by the Board of Directors in accordance with the Corporation's purposes

No assets shall be distributed to any director, officer, or private individual.

### Section 11.3 - Compliance with Law
The dissolution and distribution of assets shall comply with all applicable federal and state laws and regulations.

---

## ARTICLE XII: AMENDMENTS

### Section 12.1 - Amendment Process
These Bylaws may be amended, altered, or repealed by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the entire Board of Directors at any meeting at which a quorum is present, provided that:

a) Notice of the proposed amendment is given to all directors at least ten (10) days prior to the meeting

b) The proposed amendment is set forth in writing and included with the notice

### Section 12.2 - Limitations on Amendments
No amendment shall be adopted that would:

a) Cause the Corporation to cease to qualify for tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code

b) Violate any applicable federal or state law

c) Conflict with the Articles of Incorporation

### Section 12.3 - IRS Notification
The Corporation shall notify the IRS of any material changes to its organizing documents or operations as required by applicable regulations.

---

## ARTICLE XIII: MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

### Section 13.1 - Governing Law
These Bylaws shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wyoming and applicable federal law.

### Section 13.2 - Nondiscrimination
The Corporation shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic in its programs, activities, or employment.

### Section 13.3 - Political Activities
The Corporation shall not participate or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office. The Corporation may engage in lobbying activities only to the extent permitted for 501(c)(3) organizations under the Internal Revenue Code.

### Section 13.4 - Prohibited Activities
The Corporation shall not:
- Engage in any activities that jeopardize its tax-exempt status
- Provide excessive benefits to private individuals or entities
- Engage in substantial unrelated business activities
- Violate the prohibition on private inurement or private benefit

### Section 13.5 - Construction
If any provision of these Bylaws is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Section 13.6 - Headings
Headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of these Bylaws.
