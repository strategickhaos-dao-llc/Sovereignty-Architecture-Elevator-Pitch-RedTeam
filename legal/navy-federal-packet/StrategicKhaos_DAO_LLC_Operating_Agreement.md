### 2.2 Admission of Additional Members

- Compliance with applicable securities laws

### 2.3 Transfers of Membership Interests

## ARTICLE III: CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

### 3.1 Initial Capital Contributions
Each Member's initial capital contribution is set forth in Section 2.1 above. Capital accounts shall be maintained in accordance with Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv).

### 3.2 Additional Capital Contributions
No Member shall be required to make additional capital contributions unless agreed upon in writing by Members holding at least two-thirds (2/3) of the membership interests.

### 3.3 Distributions
Distributions shall be made to Members in proportion to their membership interests, at such times and in such amounts as determined by the Managing Member(s), subject to:
- Maintenance of adequate working capital
- Compliance with contractual obligations
- Restrictions under Wyoming law regarding distributions

### 3.4 Allocation of Profits and Losses
Profits and losses shall be allocated to Members in proportion to their membership interests, unless otherwise provided in a written agreement.

## ARTICLE IV: MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 Member-Managed Structure
The Company shall be managed by its Members (the "Member-Managed Structure"), with each Member having voting rights proportional to their membership interest.

### 4.2 Managing Member Powers
The Managing Member (initially Domenic G. Garza) shall have the authority to:
- Conduct day-to-day business operations
- Enter into contracts and agreements on behalf of the Company
- Hire and terminate employees and contractors
- Open and maintain bank accounts, including business accounts with Navy Federal Credit Union
- Execute banking resolutions and related financial documents
- Make decisions regarding Company operations, subject to limitations in Section 4.3

### 4.3 Major Decisions Requiring Member Approval
The following actions require approval by Members holding at least two-thirds (2/3) of membership interests:
- Amendments to this Operating Agreement
- Admission of new Members
- Sale, lease, or disposition of substantially all Company assets
- Merger, consolidation, or dissolution of the Company
- Incurrence of debt exceeding $50,000
- Entry into agreements exceeding $100,000 in value

### 4.4 Meetings and Voting
- **Regular Meetings:** Held at least quarterly, or as needed
- **Special Meetings:** May be called by any Member holding at least 10% interest
- **Notice:** At least 10 days' written notice required
- **Quorum:** Members holding a majority of membership interests
- **Virtual Meetings:** Permitted via video conference or other electronic means

## ARTICLE VI: FISCAL YEAR AND ACCOUNTING

### 6.1 Fiscal Year
The Company's fiscal year shall be the calendar year ending December 31.

### 6.2 Books and Records
The Company shall maintain books and records at its principal office. Members shall have the right to inspect and copy Company records upon reasonable notice.

### 6.3 Tax Treatment
The Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity for federal tax purposes (if single-member) or as a partnership (if multi-member), unless an election is made to be taxed as a corporation.

### 6.4 Tax Matters Partner
The Managing Member shall serve as the Tax Matters Partner (or Partnership Representative under the Bipartisan Budget Act) for federal tax purposes.

## ARTICLE VII: AI GOVERNANCE PROVISIONS

### 7.1 AI-Assisted Decision Making
The Company may utilize artificial intelligence systems to assist with:
- Data analysis and reporting
- Project management and workflow automation
- Research and development processes
- Customer service and support functions
- Strategic planning recommendations

### 7.2 Human Oversight Requirement
All material decisions affecting the Company must be reviewed and approved by human Members. AI systems serve in an advisory capacity only and do not have binding decision-making authority.

### 7.3 AI Ethics and Compliance
The Company shall implement and maintain AI governance policies that:
- Ensure transparency in AI-assisted processes
- Protect privacy and data security
- Prevent discriminatory or biased outcomes
- Comply with applicable AI regulations and industry standards
- Maintain audit trails for AI-assisted decisions

### 7.4 AI System Accountability
The Managing Member is responsible for:
- Oversight of AI system implementation and operation
- Regular audits of AI system performance and compliance
- Addressing any issues or concerns related to AI usage
- Ensuring AI systems align with Company values and legal obligations

## ARTICLE VIII: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 8.1 Ownership of IP
All intellectual property created by or on behalf of the Company, including but not limited to software, algorithms, documentation, and trade secrets, shall be owned by the Company.

### 8.2 Member IP Contributions

### 8.3 Protection of Trade Secrets
Members agree to maintain the confidentiality of Company trade secrets and proprietary information during and after their membership in the Company.

### 8.4 Trademark Applications
The Managing Member is authorized to file trademark applications on behalf of the Company for marks including but not limited to:

## ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 Indemnification of Members and Managers
The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless Members and managers from any liability, loss, or expense incurred in connection with Company business, except for:
- Willful misconduct or gross negligence
- Breaches of fiduciary duty
- Violations of law
- Actions taken in bad faith or not in the Company's best interest

### 9.2 Insurance
The Company may obtain liability insurance to cover Members, managers, and officers to the extent available and affordable.

## ARTICLE X: DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

### 10.1 Events of Dissolution
The Company shall dissolve upon:
- Written agreement of Members holding at least two-thirds (2/3) of membership interests
- Entry of a decree of judicial dissolution
- Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue
- Any other event specified in the Wyoming LLC Act

### 10.2 Winding Up
Upon dissolution, the Company shall:
1. Cease operations except as necessary for winding up
2. Pay or provide for debts and obligations
3. Distribute remaining assets to Members in proportion to positive capital account balances

### 10.3 Certificate of Dissolution
The Managing Member shall file a Certificate of Dissolution with the Wyoming Secretary of State.

## ARTICLE XI: MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

### 11.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wyoming.

### 11.2 Amendments
This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement of Members holding at least two-thirds (2/3) of membership interests.

### 11.3 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### 11.4 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Members regarding the Company and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

### 11.5 Binding Effect
This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Members and their respective heirs, successors, and permitted assigns.

### 11.6 Notices
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered via:
- Personal delivery
- Certified mail, return receipt requested
- Email with confirmation of receipt
- Other electronic means agreed upon by the Members

### 11.7 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one agreement.

### 11.8 Electronic Signatures
Electronic signatures shall have the same force and effect as original signatures.
