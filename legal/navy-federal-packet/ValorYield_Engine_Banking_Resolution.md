## SECTION 3: NONPROFIT BANKING POWERS AND AUTHORITIES

**3.1 General Nonprofit Banking Powers**

The authorized signatories are empowered to take any and all actions necessary or appropriate in connection with the Corporation's banking relationship with Navy Federal Credit Union, including but not limited to:

a) **Deposit Authority:**
   - Deposit cash, checks, and electronic payments
   - Receive donor contributions via all available methods
   - Accept grant payments and program fees
   - Process recurring donations and automatic payments
   - Endorse checks and instruments for deposit

b) **Disbursement Authority:**
   - Pay operational expenses consistent with approved budget
   - Issue payments to vendors, contractors, and employees
   - Make grant payments to other 501(c)(3) organizations
   - Process refunds and reimbursements
   - Make program-related payments

c) **Account Maintenance:**
   - Reconcile accounts and resolve discrepancies
   - Maintain documentation for audit and tax purposes
   - Report suspicious activity as required by law
   - Update account information and contact details

d) **Electronic Banking:**
   - Use online banking, mobile banking, and telephone banking services
   - Set up automatic payments for recurring expenses (rent, utilities, subscriptions)
   - Configure integrations with accounting and donor management software

**3.2 Restricted Fund Management**

The Corporation may establish sub-accounts or tracking systems within Navy Federal accounts to manage:

a) **Restricted Funds:**
   - Grant funds with specific use restrictions
   - Donor-restricted contributions
   - Program-specific funds
   - Endowment or reserve funds

b) **Operating Funds:**
   - General operating account for unrestricted funds
   - Program service revenue
   - Unrestricted donations

All restricted funds shall be used solely for their designated purposes and tracked in accordance with nonprofit accounting standards.

**3.3 Credit Management**

With respect to nonprofit credit cards and lines of credit:

a) **Responsible Use:**
   - Credit shall be used only for legitimate nonprofit expenses
   - Credit cards shall not be used for personal expenses
   - All charges shall be documented with receipts and purpose
   - Monthly statements shall be reviewed and reconciled

b) **Authorized Users:**
   - The President and Treasurer may authorize additional credit card users (staff members)
   - Spending limits shall be established for each user
   - All users shall sign acknowledgment of nonprofit credit card policy

c) **Timely Repayment:**
   - Credit card balances shall be paid in full monthly when possible
   - Lines of credit shall be repaid according to agreed terms
   - All debt service shall be reflected in the budget

**3.4 Third-Party Service Integration**

The authorized signatories are empowered to:

a) Authorize Navy Federal Credit Union to share account information with:
   - Nonprofit accounting software (QuickBooks Nonprofit, Aplos, Sage Intacct)
   - Donor management systems (Donorbox, Classy, Network for Good)
   - Payment processors (Stripe, PayPal Giving Fund)
   - Automation platforms (Zapier for nonprofit workflows)
   - Grant management systems

b) Execute agreements necessary for such integrations, subject to appropriate security, privacy, and data protection measures

c) Configure electronic data feeds and API access as supported by Navy Federal Credit Union

## SECTION 4: COMPLIANCE WITH 501(c)(3) REQUIREMENTS

**4.1 Charitable Purpose Commitment**

The Corporation certifies that all funds deposited in Navy Federal Credit Union accounts are held and used exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes consistent with Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

**4.2 No Private Inurement**

The Corporation represents that:

a) No part of net earnings inures to the benefit of private individuals
b) Compensation paid to officers, directors, and employees is reasonable
c) Transactions with related parties are at arm's length
d) The Corporation complies with IRS regulations on private benefit

**4.3 Unrelated Business Income**

If the Corporation engages in activities that may generate unrelated business income (UBI):

a) Such income shall be properly identified and tracked
b) Separate accounting shall be maintained if required
c) IRS Form 990-T shall be filed if UBI exceeds $1,000
d) The Board shall review UBI activities to ensure compliance

**4.4 Donor Contribution Handling**

The Corporation shall:

a) Issue timely written acknowledgments for donations of $250 or more
b) Comply with IRS substantiation requirements
c) Not provide goods or services in exchange for contributions (quid pro quo) without proper disclosure
d) Maintain donor records in accordance with IRS regulations

**4.5 Grant Compliance**

For grant funds received:

a) Funds shall be used solely for the purposes specified by the grantor
b) Required reports shall be submitted timely
c) Separate tracking shall be maintained for each grant
d) Unused funds shall be returned to grantor if required

## SECTION 5: FINANCIAL REPORTING AND ACCOUNTABILITY

**5.1 Treasurer Responsibilities**

The Treasurer shall:

a) Provide monthly financial reports to the Board of Directors
b) Maintain accurate records of all transactions
c) Ensure compliance with donor restrictions and grant requirements
d) Prepare annual financial statements
e) Coordinate annual audit or financial review (if required)
f) Ensure timely filing of IRS Form 990

**5.2 Board Oversight**

The Board of Directors shall:

a) Review and approve the annual budget
b) Review financial reports at each Board meeting
c) Monitor compliance with financial policies
d) Ensure adequate internal controls
e) Review and approve significant expenditures

**5.3 Annual Audit/Review**

If required by law, regulation, or Board policy, the Corporation shall engage an independent CPA to conduct:

a) An annual audit (for larger organizations)
b) An annual review (for medium-sized organizations)
c) A compilation with independent oversight (for smaller organizations)

**5.4 IRS Filing Requirements**

The Corporation shall file:

a) IRS Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N (e-Postcard) annually
b) State charitable registration renewals as required
c) IRS Form 990-T if unrelated business income exceeds $1,000
d) Other returns and reports as required by federal and state law

The Corporation may implement fund management strategies including:

b) **Reserve Account (NFCU or other institution):**
   - Emergency reserves (3-6 months operating expenses)

a) FDIC or NCUA insured accounts
