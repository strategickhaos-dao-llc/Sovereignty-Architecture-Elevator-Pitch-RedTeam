## PART III - REQUIRED PROVISIONS IN ORGANIZING DOCUMENT

The organizing document must include:

### 1. Purpose Clause
```
The organization is organized exclusively for charitable and educational 
purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, 
including but not limited to:

(a) Receiving and distributing funds to highly effective charitable 
    organizations focused on global health, poverty alleviation, and 
    disaster relief;

(b) Providing grants to organizations recommended by independent charity 
    evaluators such as GiveWell;

(c) Advancing public education about effective charitable giving and 
    cryptocurrency-based philanthropy.
```

### 2. Dissolution Clause
```
Upon dissolution of the organization, assets shall be distributed for one 
or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the 
Internal Revenue Code, or shall be distributed to the federal government, 
or to a state or local government, for a public purpose.
```

### 3. Private Inurement Prohibition
```
No part of the net earnings of the organization shall inure to the benefit 
of, or be distributable to its members, trustees, officers, or other private 
persons, except that the organization shall be authorized to pay reasonable 
compensation for services rendered.
```

## PART IV - NARRATIVE DESCRIPTION OF ACTIVITIES

### Primary Activities

**1. Charitable Fund Distribution (80% of activities)**

The organization receives funds from the Strategickhaos DAO LLC through an 
automatic smart contract mechanism. Seven percent (7%) of all DAO revenues 
are irrevocably directed to this organization.

Funds received are distributed quarterly to pre-approved, highly effective 
charitable organizations. The organization uses GiveWell's research to 
select recipient charities based on:
- Cost-effectiveness analysis
- Transparency and accountability
- Room for additional funding
- Evidence of impact

**Current designated beneficiaries:**
- Against Malaria Foundation (25%)
- Helen Keller International (25%)
- Malaria Consortium (25%)
- New Incentives (25%)

**2. Public Education (15% of activities)**

The organization maintains public transparency reports showing:
- All funds received (on-chain and off-chain)
- All distributions made
- Impact metrics from recipient charities
- Educational content about effective altruism

**3. Administrative (5% of activities)**

- Regulatory compliance
- Annual reporting
- IRS filings
- Donor communications

## FILING INSTRUCTIONS

1. **Obtain EIN First**
   - File IRS Form SS-4 online at: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online

2. **Complete Form 1023**
   - Use IRS Pay.gov portal: https://www.pay.gov/public/form/start/62759871

3. **Pay Filing Fee**
   - $600 for organizations expecting gross receipts > $10,000

4. **Submit and Wait**
   - Processing typically takes 3-6 months
   - IRS may request additional information

5. **Receive Determination Letter**
   - Keep this letter permanently
   - Effective date will be date of formation if filed within 27 months
