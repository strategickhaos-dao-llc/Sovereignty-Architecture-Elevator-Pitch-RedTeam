This Operating Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into by and among the Members identified in Exhibit A, for the purpose of governing the affairs of STRATEGICKHAOS CHARITABLE DAO LLC, a Wyoming decentralized autonomous organization (the "DAO" or "Company").

---

## ARTICLE 1 - FORMATION AND NAME

### 1.1 Formation
The Company was formed as a Wyoming limited liability company by the filing of Articles of Organization with the Wyoming Secretary of State pursuant to the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act (W.S. § 17-29-101 et seq.) and the Wyoming Decentralized Autonomous Organization Supplement (W.S. § 17-31-101 et seq.).

### 1.2 Name
The name of the Company is STRATEGICKHAOS CHARITABLE DAO LLC.

### 1.3 DAO Designation
The Company is a decentralized autonomous organization as defined by Wyoming law. The Company's governance and operations are facilitated by smart contracts deployed on the Base blockchain network.

---

## ARTICLE 2 - PURPOSE AND POWERS

### 2.1 Purpose
The Company is formed for the following purposes:

(a) To provide cybersecurity research, education, consulting, and related services;

(b) To develop and maintain open-source software for digital sovereignty;

(c) To perpetually allocate seven percent (7%) of all gross revenues to qualified 501(c)(3) charitable organizations; and

(d) To engage in any lawful act or activity permitted under Wyoming law.

### 2.2 Powers
The Company shall have all powers necessary or convenient to carry out its purposes, including but not limited to:

(a) Entering into contracts and agreements;

(b) Acquiring, holding, and disposing of property;

(c) Deploying and managing smart contracts on blockchain networks;

(d) Hiring employees and engaging contractors;

(e) Establishing bank accounts and cryptocurrency wallets.

## ARTICLE 5 - MANAGEMENT

### 5.1 Member-Managed
The Company shall be managed by its Members. Each Member shall have authority to bind the Company for ordinary business matters.

### 5.2 Major Decisions
The following decisions require approval by Members holding at least sixty-six percent (66%) of Membership Interests:

(a) Amendment of this Agreement (except Article 6);

(b) Merger or dissolution of the Company;

(c) Sale of substantially all Company assets;

(d) Admission or expulsion of Members;

(e) Incurrence of debt exceeding $50,000.

### 5.3 Voting Mechanism
Voting may be conducted:

(a) At meetings called with at least 48 hours notice;

(b) By written consent;

(c) Via on-chain governance mechanisms approved by the Members.

---

## ARTICLE 6 - IRREVOCABLE CHARITABLE COMMITMENT

**⚠️ THIS ARTICLE MAY NOT BE AMENDED, MODIFIED, OR REMOVED UNDER ANY CIRCUMSTANCES, INCLUDING BY UNANIMOUS MEMBER VOTE. THIS ARTICLE SURVIVES ANY ATTEMPT TO AMEND OR RESTATE THIS AGREEMENT.**

### 6.1 Mandatory Charitable Allocation
The Company shall perpetually allocate seven percent (7%) of all gross revenues to the Charitable Fund, as defined in Section 6.2.

### 6.2 Charitable Fund
The "Charitable Fund" means funds held by the MerkleCharityDistributor smart contract or any successor charitable vehicle designated by the Company, provided that such vehicle:

(a) Is a qualified 501(c)(3) organization; and

(b) Maintains the charitable purpose specified in Section 6.3.

### 6.3 Charitable Purpose
The Charitable Fund shall distribute funds exclusively to:

(a) GiveWell top-rated charities;

(b) Other 501(c)(3) organizations focused on global health, poverty alleviation, or disaster relief;

(c) As a last resort, community foundations that serve similar purposes.

### 6.4 Smart Contract Enforcement
The primary mechanism for the charitable allocation shall be the IrrevocableCharitySplitter smart contract, which shall:

(a) Automatically receive all Company revenues;

(b) Immediately forward 93% to the Company's operational wallet;

(c) Immediately forward 7% to the Charitable Fund.

### 6.5 Backup Enforcement
If the smart contract mechanism fails for any reason, the Members are jointly and severally personally obligated to ensure the 7% allocation is made within thirty (30) calendar days.

### 6.6 Charitable Remainder
Upon dissolution of the Company, all remaining assets after payment of debts and liabilities shall be distributed to qualified 501(c)(3) organizations consistent with Section 6.3.

### 6.7 Member Acknowledgment
By executing this Agreement, each Member acknowledges:

(a) They have read and understood this Article 6;

(b) This Article is material consideration for their membership;

(c) This Article creates legally binding obligations that cannot be waived;

(d) Violation of this Article may result in personal liability.

## ARTICLE 7 - CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS AND ALLOCATIONS

### 7.1 Initial Contributions
The initial capital contributions of the Members are set forth in Exhibit A.

### 7.2 Additional Contributions
No Member shall be required to make additional capital contributions.

### 7.3 Allocations
After the 7% charitable allocation required by Article 6, profits and losses shall be allocated to Members in proportion to their Membership Interests.

### 7.4 Distributions
Distributions to Members shall be made at the discretion of the Members, provided that:

(a) The 7% charitable allocation has been satisfied; and

(b) The Company has adequate reserves for operations.

## ARTICLE 8 - RECORDS AND ACCOUNTING

### 8.1 Books and Records
The Company shall maintain:

(a) This Agreement and all amendments;

(b) Financial records and tax returns;

(c) Records of all smart contract deployments and transactions;

(d) Records of all charitable distributions.

### 8.2 On-Chain Transparency
The Company shall maintain public on-chain records of:

(a) All funds received by the charity splitter;

(b) All distributions to the charitable fund;

(c) All distributions from the charitable fund to named charities.

### 8.3 Annual Report
The Company shall prepare an annual report detailing:

(a) Total revenues received;

(b) Total charitable distributions (7%);

(c) Named charities receiving distributions;

(d) Impact metrics where available.

---

## ARTICLE 9 - INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 Indemnification
The Company shall indemnify each Member, manager, and agent against all losses, liabilities, and expenses arising from their role in the Company, except for:

(a) Willful misconduct;

(b) Knowing violation of law;

(c) Breach of this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE 10 - DISSOLUTION

### 10.1 Events Causing Dissolution
The Company shall be dissolved upon:

(a) Vote of Members holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of Membership Interests;

(b) Entry of a judicial decree of dissolution;

(c) Any other event causing dissolution under Wyoming law.

### 10.2 Winding Up
Upon dissolution:

(a) First, pay all debts and liabilities;

(b) Second, distribute remaining assets to charitable organizations per Section 6.6;

(c) Third, if charitable distribution is legally impossible, distribute to Members.

### 11.1 Governing Law
