## üí° Pro Tips for Getting "Yes" Faster

### 1. **Lead with Public Benefit**
Lawyers love projects that create precedent and help the broader community. Emphasize:
- "This creates a template for other DAOs"
- "Open source documentation helps the entire ecosystem"
- "Public benefit for education (SNHU)"

### 2. **Make It Easy**
- Have all docs ready in one GitHub repo
- Provide clear, specific ask (e.g., "review Section 3 of royalty assignment")
- Set reasonable timeline (1-2 hours, not 20)

### 3. **Offer Credit**
- Public recognition in HALL-OF-FAME.md
- Twitter/LinkedIn shout-out
- Citation in legal documentation

### 4. **Show You've Done Your Homework**
- Reference specific forms (8283, 8899)
- Mention relevant law (SF0068, IRC 170)
- Demonstrate understanding of basic concepts

### 5. **Time It Right**
- Law schools: September-November, February-April (avoid exam periods)
- Big law: Avoid December (billable hours push) and August (vacations)
- Always: Tuesday-Thursday morning emails get best response rates
