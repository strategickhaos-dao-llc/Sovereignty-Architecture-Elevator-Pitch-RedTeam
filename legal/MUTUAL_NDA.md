## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship relating to cybersecurity consulting, private investigation, and/or security services ("Purpose"); and

WHEREAS, in connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other Party;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 "Confidential Information" means:

All non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

- (a) Technical information: inventions, trade secrets, know-how, software, algorithms, source code, security vulnerabilities, system architecture, and methodologies
- (b) Business information: financial data, business plans, pricing, customer lists, vendor relationships, and marketing strategies
- (c) Security findings: vulnerability assessments, penetration testing results, incident reports, and remediation recommendations
- (d) Personal information: employee data, client data, and any information subject to privacy regulations

### 1.2 Confidential Information includes:

- Information marked as "Confidential" or similar designation
- Information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature and circumstances of disclosure
- All notes, analyses, summaries, and other materials derived from Confidential Information

---

## 2. EXCLUSIONS

Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

- (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party
- (b) Was rightfully in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure, as evidenced by written records
- (c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information
- (d) Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure
- (e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice to enable the Disclosing Party to seek protective measures

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party agrees to:

### 3.1 Confidentiality

- (a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
- (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent of the Disclosing Party
- (c) Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose

### 3.2 Protection

- (a) Protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care
- (b) Limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, and agents who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement
- (c) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party upon discovery of any unauthorized use or disclosure

### 3.3 No Copies

- (a) Not copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as necessary for the Purpose
- (b) Maintain records of all copies made

---

## 4. SECURITY TESTING PROVISIONS

### 4.1 Special Provisions for Security Engagements

Given the nature of cybersecurity services, the Parties acknowledge that:

- (a) Provider may access and analyze Client systems, code, and data as authorized in the Master Services Agreement
- (b) Security findings, vulnerabilities, and testing results are highly sensitive Confidential Information
- (c) Unauthorized disclosure of security findings could cause significant harm

### 4.2 Enhanced Protections

For security-related Confidential Information:

- (a) Access shall be limited to personnel directly involved in the engagement
- (b) All findings shall be transmitted via encrypted channels
- (c) Client systems shall not be accessed from unsecured networks
- (d) No security findings shall be published or presented without written consent

---

## 5. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

### 5.1 Upon Request or Termination

Upon the Disclosing Party's written request or upon termination of this Agreement:

- (a) The Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information
- (b) The Receiving Party shall certify in writing that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed
- (c) The Receiving Party may retain one archival copy solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to ongoing confidentiality obligations

### 5.2 Automatic Obligation

If no business relationship results within one (1) year of disclosure, the Receiving Party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information without request.
