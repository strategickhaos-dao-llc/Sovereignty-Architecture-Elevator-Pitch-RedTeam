## Overview

The 36 Laws of Impulse represent a comprehensive governance framework for managing, protecting, and optimizing impulse-driven cognitive processes. These laws are particularly relevant for neurodivergent thinking patterns (ADHD, autism, etc.) and form the foundation of both the Impulse Armor system and the Sovereign Expression Shield.

## Structure

The laws are organized into four categories of nine laws each:

1. **Foundation Laws (1-9)**: Recognition and categorization
2. **Processing Laws (10-18)**: Execution and management
3. **Protection Laws (19-27)**: Defense and resilience
4. **Optimization Laws (28-36)**: Enhancement and output

---

## Foundation Laws (1-9)
### Recognition and Categorization

**Law 1: Impulse Recognition**  
Every impulse must be acknowledged as valid data, not dismissed or suppressed.

**Law 2: Impulse Classification**  
Categorize impulses by type: creative, protective, exploratory, communicative, or systemic.

**Law 3: Impulse Urgency Assessment**  
Evaluate true urgency versus perceived urgency. Not all impulses require immediate action.

**Law 4: Impulse Source Identification**  
Identify whether impulse originates from internal cognition, external stimulus, or systemic pattern.

**Law 5: Impulse Context Mapping**  
Map impulse to current context, goals, and environmental conditions.

**Law 6: Impulse Validity Testing**  
Test impulse against reality constraints and known patterns before acting.

**Law 7: Impulse Priority Queuing**  
Establish priority queue for multiple simultaneous impulses based on urgency and alignment.

**Law 8: Impulse Pattern Recognition**  
Identify recurring impulse patterns and their historical outcomes.

**Law 9: Impulse Acceptance**  
Accept impulses as part of cognitive architecture rather than fighting natural patterns.

---

## Processing Laws (10-18)
### Execution and Management

**Law 10: Parallel Processing Permission**  
Allow multiple impulses to be processed simultaneously when cognitive capacity permits.

**Law 11: Interrupt Handling Protocol**  
Establish graceful interrupt handling for new impulses during active processing.

**Law 12: Context Switching Efficiency**  
Minimize cognitive cost of switching between impulse contexts through state preservation.

**Law 13: Hyperfocus Channeling**  
When hyperfocus emerges, channel it toward highest-value targets aligned with goals.

**Law 14: Impulse Completion Tracking**  
Track impulse execution to completion to prevent cognitive load from incomplete loops.

**Law 15: Impulse Delegation Authority**  
Authorize delegation of impulses to external systems, tools, or future processing.

**Law 16: Impulse Batching Strategy**  
Group similar impulses for efficient batch processing when appropriate.

**Law 17: Impulse Time Boxing**  
Allocate time limits to impulse exploration to prevent infinite recursion.

**Law 18: Impulse Output Validation**  
Verify that impulse execution produces intended outcomes before closing loop.

## Protection Laws (19-27)
### Defense and Resilience

**Law 19: Cognitive Load Monitoring**  
Continuously monitor total cognitive load to prevent overwhelm state.

**Law 20: Overwhelm Prevention Protocol**  
Activate protective mechanisms before overwhelm threshold is reached.

**Law 21: Impulse Armor Activation**  
Deploy Impulse Armor when external demands exceed processing capacity.

**Law 22: Recovery Protocol Initiation**  
Trigger recovery protocols when cognitive burnout indicators appear.

**Law 23: Boundary Establishment**  
Set and enforce boundaries on impulse quantity and intensity during vulnerable states.

**Law 24: Defensive Suppression**  
Authorize temporary suppression of non-critical impulses during high-load periods.

**Law 25: External Interrupt Filtering**  
Filter external interrupts to protect cognitive processing from disruption.

**Law 26: State Preservation**  
Preserve cognitive state before and during protection protocol activation.

**Law 27: Resilience Building**  
Use controlled exposure to build long-term resilience to impulse overwhelm.
