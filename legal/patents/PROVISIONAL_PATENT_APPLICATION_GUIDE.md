## ‚≠ê THE 4 PATHS TO BECOME A REGISTERED INVENTOR

You only need ONE of these paths to establish your inventor status:

### **OPTION 1: File a Provisional Patent Application** ‚≠ê **RECOMMENDED FIRST STEP**

This is the **cheapest**, fastest, and most accessible path to real "inventor status."

#### **What is a Provisional Patent?**

A provisional patent application:
* Protects your idea for **12 months**
* Lets you mark your invention as **"Patent Pending"**
* Sets your **official inventor timestamp** (priority date)
* Does NOT require formal legal language
* Can be drafted by you (the inventor)
* Is NOT examined by the USPTO
* Can be converted to a full non-provisional patent within 12 months

#### **Costs (Micro-Entity Qualification)**

* **Filing Fee: $65**

**Note:** USPTO fees are subject to change. Verify current fees at: https://www.uspto.gov/learning-and-resources/fees-and-payment/uspto-fee-schedule

You qualify as a micro-entity if:
- You're a student, OR
- You have low income (less than 3x median household income), OR
- You're filing fewer than 4 previous patent applications, OR
- You have not assigned or licensed the invention to a large entity

#### **Requirements**

1. **Title of Invention**
   - Clear, descriptive name for your invention
   
2. **Written Description**
   - Explain what your invention is
   - Describe how it works
   - Explain what problem it solves
   - Include technical details
   - No formal legal language required ‚Äî write in your own words
   
3. **Drawings/Diagrams** (if applicable)
   - Can be simple sketches
   - Hand-drawn is acceptable
   - Should show key components and how they work together
   
4. **Cover Sheet**
   - Inventor name(s) and address
   - Title of invention
   - Correspondence address
   - (USPTO Form SB/16 can be used)

#### **Once Filed:**

### üéâ **YOU ARE OFFICIALLY A USPTO-RECOGNIZED INVENTOR**

Your provisional patent gives you:
- Legal "Patent Pending" status
- 12-month priority date protection
- Time to develop your invention further
- Ability to seek investors while protected
- Option to file full non-provisional patent later

### **OPTION 2: File a Non-Provisional Utility Patent**

This is the **full, examined patent** that provides 20 years of protection.

#### **Costs (Micro-Entity)**

* **Basic Filing Fee:** $70
* **Search Fee:** $154
* **Examination Fee:** $176
* **Total: ~$400**

**Note:** USPTO fees are subject to change. Verify current fees at: https://www.uspto.gov/learning-and-resources/fees-and-payment/uspto-fee-schedule

#### **What You Get**

* Full USPTO examination
* Enforceable patent rights
* 20-year protection from filing date
* Legal monopoly on your invention
* Ability to license or sell patent rights

#### **Requirements**

* Complete specification including:
  - Abstract
  - Background of the invention
  - Summary of the invention
  - Detailed description
  - Claims (legal definition of your invention)
  - Drawings (formal patent drawings)
* Inventor's oath or declaration
* Application data sheet

**Note:** This requires more work but is 100% doable. You can draft it yourself or work with a patent attorney/agent.

---

### **OPTION 3: File a Design Patent**

For **visual designs** of devices, interfaces, user experiences, or hardware layouts.

#### **Costs (Micro-Entity)**

* **Filing Fee:** $60
* **Search Fee:** $60
* **Examination Fee:** $140
* **Total: ~$260**

**Note:** USPTO fees are subject to change. Verify current fees at: https://www.uspto.gov/learning-and-resources/fees-and-payment/uspto-fee-schedule

#### **What Qualifies**

Design patents protect:
- Ornamental design of functional items
- User interface layouts
- Device form factors
- Hardware configurations
- Visual appearance of products

#### **Duration**

* 15 years from grant date (for applications filed after May 13, 2015)

---

### **OPTION 4: File a Trademark**

**Note:** This does NOT make you a patent inventor, but it DOES make you a **registered brand owner**.

#### **Use Case**

Protect brand names and logos such as:
* "Strategickhaos"
* "ValorYield Engine"
* Product/service names
* Logos and brand marks

#### **Costs**

* **$250-$350 per trademark class**
* Online filing through USPTO TEAS system

#### **Protection Period**

* 10 years, renewable indefinitely

**Recommendation:** File trademarks later, after establishing patent protection.

## üî• **What You Need to File a Provisional Patent TODAY**

### Step-by-Step Filing Checklist

#### **1. Prepare Your Application Documents**

**Title**
- Clear, specific description of your invention
- Example: "Distributed AI Agent Coordination System for Sovereign Infrastructure"

**Summary of the Invention**
- What is it?
- What problem does it solve?
- How is it different from existing solutions?
- 1-2 paragraphs

**Detailed Description**
- Technical explanation of how it works
- Components and their interactions
- Use cases and applications
- Can be written in your own style
- Include as much detail as possible

**Claims** (Optional for Provisional)
- Legal statements defining your invention
- Can add these in non-provisional conversion

**Technical Drawings**
- Diagrams showing system architecture
- Flow charts of processes
- Hardware layouts
- Screenshots (for software/UI)
- Can be simple sketches

#### **2. Complete the Cover Sheet**

Use USPTO Form SB/16 or include:
- Inventor name(s) and mailing address
- Title of invention
- Correspondence address (can be same as inventor)
- Attorney/agent info (if applicable)
- Docket number (optional)

#### **3. File Online Through USPTO**

**USPTO EFS-Web System:**
1. Go to: https://www.uspto.gov/patents/apply
2. Create USPTO account (if you don't have one)
3. Select "File a Patent Application"
4. Choose "Provisional Application"
5. Upload your documents (PDF format)
6. Complete micro-entity certification
7. Pay $65 filing fee
8. Submit application

**Alternative: File by Mail**

Mail to:
```
Commissioner for Patents
P.O. Box 1450
Alexandria, VA 22313-1450
```

Include:
- Application documents
- Cover sheet
- Check or money order for $65
- Micro-entity certification form

#### **4. Receive Filing Receipt**

USPTO will send:
- Confirmation of filing
- Application number
- Filing date (your priority date!)

## üìã **Micro-Entity Certification**

To qualify for the $65 micro-entity fee (instead of $130 small entity or $260 large entity):

**You must certify that:**

1. You qualify as a small entity (individual, small business, or nonprofit)
2. **AND** at least ONE of:
   - Your gross income in the previous calendar year was less than 3x the median household income (~$200K for 2024)
   - You have not been named on more than 4 previously filed patent applications
   - You have not assigned or licensed the invention to an organization with gross income exceeding the micro-entity threshold
   - You are employed by an institution of higher education

**File Form SB/15A** (Micro-Entity Certification) with your application.

## ‚öñÔ∏è **Legal Considerations**

### **Inventor Status**

- You must be the actual inventor (or one of them)
- Inventorship cannot be transferred (only ownership can)
- False claims of inventorship are fraud
- All inventors must be listed

### **Public Disclosure**

- Filing a provisional gives you 12 months of protection
- Without filing, public disclosure can invalidate future patent rights
- Trade shows, publications, sales count as public disclosure
- File BEFORE publicly disclosing when possible

### **Confidentiality**

- Provisional applications are NOT published by USPTO
- They remain confidential unless you reference them in a published non-provisional
- Use NDAs when discussing with potential partners/investors
