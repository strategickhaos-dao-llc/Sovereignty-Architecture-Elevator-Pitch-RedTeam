# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF STRATEGICKHAOS DAO LLC

**A Wyoming Limited Liability Company**

*Effective Date: [Date]*

---

## ARTICLE I - ORGANIZATION

### Section 1.1 Formation
The Company has been organized as a Wyoming limited liability company by the filing of Articles of Organization with the Wyoming Secretary of State in accordance with the Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act.

### Section 1.2 Name
The name of the Company shall be "Strategickhaos DAO LLC" or such other name as the Members may from time to time determine.

### Section 1.3 Purpose
The Company is organized for the purpose of engaging in any lawful business activity, including but not limited to providing private investigation, cybersecurity, and security consulting services.

---

## ARTICLE II - MEMBERS AND GOVERNANCE

### Section 2.1 Management Structure
The Company shall be member-managed. All Members shall have the right to participate in the management and conduct of the Company's business.

### Section 2.2 Voting Rights
Each Member shall have voting rights proportional to their Membership Interest. Major decisions affecting the Company shall require approval by a majority vote of the Members, with each Member's vote weighted according to their Membership Interest percentage.

### Section 2.3 Veto Powers
The Managing Member shall have veto power over any proposed action that, in the Managing Member's reasonable judgment, would violate applicable law or the Company's fiduciary duties to Members or third parties.

### Section 2.4 Quorum Requirements
A quorum for any meeting of the Members shall consist of Members holding at least sixty percent (60%) of the total Membership Interests. No action may be taken at a meeting without a quorum being present.

---

## ARTICLE III - FINANCIAL PROVISIONS

### Section 3.1 Capital Contributions
Each Member shall make initial and additional capital contributions as determined by the Members. All capital contributions shall be recorded in the Company's books and records.

### Section 3.2 Charitable Allocations
The Company shall allocate a minimum of five percent (5%) of its annual net profits to charitable organizations or community initiatives as determined by a majority vote of the Members. This allocation must be made within ninety (90) days following the close of each fiscal year.

### Section 3.3 Distribution of Profits
Net profits shall be distributed to Members in proportion to their Membership Interests, subject to the charitable allocation requirement in Section 3.2 and any reserves determined by the Members to be necessary for the Company's operations.

### Section 3.4 Financial Reporting
The Managing Member shall ensure that accurate financial records are maintained and that quarterly financial statements are prepared and made available to all Members within thirty (30) days following the end of each quarter.

---

## ARTICLE IV - FIDUCIARY DUTIES AND RISK MANAGEMENT

### Section 4.1 Duty of Care
Each Member and Manager owes a duty of care to the Company and the other Members. Members and Managers shall discharge their duties in good faith, with the care an ordinarily prudent person in a like position would exercise under similar circumstances.

### Section 4.2 Duty of Loyalty
Members and Managers owe a duty of loyalty to the Company and shall not compete with the Company or usurp Company opportunities without the informed consent of a majority of the disinterested Members.

### Section 4.3 Risk Assessment
Before entering into any transaction or commitment involving more than $10,000 or that could materially affect the Company's operations, the Managing Member shall conduct and document a risk assessment. The risk assessment must evaluate financial, legal, and operational risks.

### Section 4.4 Conflict of Interest
Any Member who has a material personal interest in a transaction involving the Company must disclose that interest prior to any vote on the transaction. Such Member shall abstain from voting on the matter unless authorized to vote by a majority of the disinterested Members.

---

## ARTICLE V - RECORDS AND TRANSPARENCY

### Section 5.1 Books and Records
The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of all financial transactions, Member contributions, distributions, and governance decisions. All records shall be kept at the Company's principal place of business.

### Section 5.2 Audit Rights
Each Member shall have the right to inspect and audit the Company's books and records during reasonable business hours upon reasonable prior notice. The Company shall cooperate fully with any such inspection or audit.

### Section 5.3 Audit Trail Requirements
All significant decisions, votes, and financial transactions must be documented with an audit trail that includes the date, participants, decision made, and rationale. This audit trail must be maintained for a minimum of seven (7) years.

### Section 5.4 Transparency Reports
The Company shall prepare and distribute to all Members an annual transparency report summarizing key decisions, financial performance, charitable allocations, and any material changes to operations or membership.

---

## ARTICLE VI - COMPLIANCE AND LEGAL REQUIREMENTS

### Section 6.1 Regulatory Compliance
The Company shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances. The Managing Member is responsible for ensuring such compliance.

### Section 6.2 Licensing Requirements
The Company shall maintain all licenses and permits required for its lawful operation, including any professional licenses required for investigation and cybersecurity services.

### Section 6.3 Data Protection
The Company shall implement and maintain reasonable security measures to protect confidential client information and personal data in accordance with applicable data protection laws. The Company shall not disclose confidential information except as authorized by law or by the client.

### Section 6.4 Prohibited Activities
The Company and its Members shall not engage in any activity that is prohibited by law, that could jeopardize the Company's licenses, or that would constitute fraud, misrepresentation, or other unethical conduct.

---

## ARTICLE VII - AMENDMENTS AND DISSOLUTION

### Section 7.1 Amendments
This Operating Agreement may be amended only by written consent of Members holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the total Membership Interests. Any amendment shall be effective only when reduced to writing and signed by the requisite Members.

### Section 7.2 Dissolution
The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following events:
1. The unanimous consent of all Members to dissolve the Company;
2. The entry of a judicial decree dissolving the Company;
3. Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue its business.

Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up in accordance with Wyoming law.

---

## ARTICLE VIII - GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 8.1 Governing Law
This Operating Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wyoming.

### Section 8.2 Entire Agreement
This Operating Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Members relating to the Company and supersedes all prior agreements, written or oral.

### Section 8.3 Severability
If any provision of this Operating Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
