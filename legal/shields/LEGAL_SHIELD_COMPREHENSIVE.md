**Protection:** Public figures must prove "actual malice" (knowledge of falsity or reckless disregard) to win defamation claims.
**Application:** Criticism of public figures, companies, or public officials is highly protected.

### 46. Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code ยง 73.001-006 - Truth Defense

**Application:** Factually accurate statements, even if damaging, are completely protected.

### 47. Texas Citizens Participation Act (TCPA) ยง 27.005 - Anti-SLAPP Fee Shifting
**Protection:** If SLAPP suit is dismissed, plaintiff must pay defendant's attorney fees and costs.

### 48. Milkovich v. Lorain Journal Co., 497 U.S. 1 (1990)

### 49. Gertz v. Robert Welch, Inc., 418 U.S. 323 (1974)

### 50. Texas Defamation Mitigation Act - Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code ยง 73.055

### 52. Hustler Magazine v. Falwell, 485 U.S. 46 (1988)

### 53. Bose Corp. v. Consumers Union, 466 U.S. 485 (1984)

### 54. Philadelphia Newspapers v. Hepps, 475 U.S. 767 (1986)
