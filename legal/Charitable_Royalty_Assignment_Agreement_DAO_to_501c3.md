## RECITALS

**A.** The Company is the owner of certain intellectual property, including but not limited to the Sovereignty Architecture, related software, documentation, and derivative works (collectively, the "IP Assets").

**B.** The Company desires to make an irrevocable charitable contribution by assigning a perpetual royalty interest in the IP Assets to the Charity.

**C.** The Charity is a qualified 501(c)(3) organization under the Internal Revenue Code and is willing to accept this assignment.

**D.** The parties intend this assignment to qualify as a completed gift of a partial interest in property that satisfies the requirements of IRC §170(f)(3) and related regulations.

## AGREEMENT

### 1. ASSIGNMENT OF ROYALTY INTEREST

**1.1 Grant of Royalty Interest**

The Company hereby assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Charity an irrevocable, perpetual royalty interest equal to seven percent (7%) of all Net Royalty Income (as defined below) derived from the IP Assets.

**1.2 Definition of Net Royalty Income**

"Net Royalty Income" means:
- All gross royalties, licensing fees, revenue sharing payments, and other income derived from the exploitation, licensing, or commercialization of the IP Assets
- **Less:** Documented third-party platform fees (e.g., GitHub Sponsors fees, payment processor fees, marketplace commissions)
- **Less:** Direct costs of IP maintenance, prosecution, and enforcement reasonably allocated to the IP Assets

**1.3 Included Rights**

This royalty interest includes income from:
- Software licensing (open source sponsorships, commercial licenses, enterprise agreements)
- Documentation and educational materials
- Consulting and professional services directly related to the IP Assets
- Derivative works and improvements
- All media, formats, and distribution channels

### 2. PAYMENT TERMS

**2.1 Payment Schedule**

The Company shall pay the Charity its royalty share on a quarterly basis, within thirty (30) days after the end of each calendar quarter.

**2.2 Accounting and Reporting**

The Company shall provide the Charity with:
- Quarterly statements detailing gross royalty income, deductions, and net royalty calculations
- Annual summary reports suitable for the Charity's IRS Form 990 reporting
- Access to relevant financial records upon reasonable request

**2.3 Minimum Payment**

No minimum payment is required in any period where no Net Royalty Income is generated.

### 3. IRREVOCABILITY AND PERPETUITY

**3.1 Irrevocable Assignment**

This assignment is **IRREVOCABLE** and may not be terminated, amended, or rescinded by either party except as specifically provided in Section 3.3.

**3.2 Binding Effect**

This assignment shall:
- Bind the Company and its successors, assigns, and any acquiring entities
- Continue in perpetuity for the duration of the IP Assets' commercial life
- Survive any merger, acquisition, sale, or restructuring of the Company

**3.3 Successor Charity**

If the Charity ceases to exist or loses its 501(c)(3) status, the Company may designate a successor qualified 501(c)(3) charity. The royalty obligation continues uninterrupted.

### 4. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

**4.1 Company Representations**

The Company represents and warrants that:
- It is a validly existing Wyoming LLC with authority to enter this Agreement
- It owns or controls the IP Assets subject to this royalty assignment
- This assignment does not violate any existing agreements or obligations
- The Managing Member has authority to execute this Agreement

**4.2 Charity Representations**

The Charity represents and warrants that:
- It is a validly existing 501(c)(3) organization in good standing
- It has authority to accept this charitable contribution
- It will use the royalty income for its exempt purposes

### 5. TAX COMPLIANCE

**5.1 Charitable Contribution Treatment**

The parties intend this assignment to qualify as a charitable contribution under IRC §170.

**5.2 Contemporaneous Written Acknowledgment**

The Charity shall provide the Company with a contemporaneous written acknowledgment satisfying IRS requirements under IRC §170(f)(8), including:
- Description of the property contributed
- Statement that no goods or services were provided in exchange (or description of any provided)
- Date of contribution

**5.3 Form 8283 Cooperation**

The Charity shall cooperate with the Company in completing IRS Form 8283 (Noncash Charitable Contributions) and any required appraisals.

**5.4 Disposition Reporting**

If the Charity disposes of the royalty interest within three years, it shall file Form 8282 as required by IRC §6050L.
