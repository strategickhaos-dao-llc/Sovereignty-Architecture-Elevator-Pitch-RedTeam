# Category 10: Miscellaneous Eternal Classics

This category contains 10 timeless papers on decentralization, economics, and the philosophy of digital sovereignty.

## Papers Included

1. **What is Property?** - Pierre-Joseph Proudhon (1840)
   - Foundational anarchist philosophy
   - "Property is theft!"

2. **Mutual Aid: A Factor of Evolution** - Pyotr Kropotkin (1902)
   - Cooperation as evolutionary advantage
   - Alternative to social Darwinism

3. **The Use of Knowledge in Society** - Friedrich Hayek (1945)
   - Distributed knowledge and coordination
   - Economic calculation debate

4. **Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System** - Satoshi Nakamoto (2008)
   - First decentralized cryptocurrency
   - Proof-of-work consensus

5. **Ethereum Whitepaper** - Vitalik Buterin (2013)
   - Smart contract platform
   - Programmable blockchain

6. **IPFS Paper** - Protocol Labs
   - Content-addressed distributed file system
   - Permanent web infrastructure

7. **Filecoin: A Decentralized Storage Network** - Protocol Labs (2017)
   - Incentivized distributed storage
   - Market-based coordination

8. **Decentralized Identity** - Various authors
   - Self-sovereign identity
   - User-controlled credentials

9. **W3C Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs)** - W3C (2021)
   - Standard for decentralized identity
   - Interoperable identity layer

10. **Web3: A Vision for a Decentralized Internet**
    - Next generation internet architecture
    - User ownership and control

## Why These Papers Matter

These are the philosophical and technical foundations of digital sovereignty:
- **Proudhon and Kropotkin** provide philosophical grounding for decentralization
- **Hayek** explains why distributed systems can coordinate without central authority
- **Bitcoin** proved decentralized consensus is possible
- **Ethereum** showed programmable decentralization
- **IPFS/Filecoin** enable permanent, distributed storage
- **DID/Web3** complete the sovereign digital identity stack

Essential for:
- Understanding the "why" of sovereignty
- Building decentralized systems
- Philosophical grounding
- Historical context
- Future vision

## Recommended Reading Order

1. **Hayek's "Use of Knowledge"** for economic foundations
2. **Bitcoin whitepaper** for technical proof of concept
3. **Ethereum whitepaper** for programmable blockchains
4. **IPFS** and **Filecoin** for storage layer
5. **W3C DIDs** for identity standards
6. **Proudhon** and **Kropotkin** for philosophical depth
