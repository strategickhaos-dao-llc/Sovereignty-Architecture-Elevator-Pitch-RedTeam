# Category 04: Law & Governance

This category contains 10 papers on legal frameworks, regulations, and governance for AI and digital systems.

## Papers Included

1. **EU AI Act** - European Parliament (2021)
   - First comprehensive AI regulation
   - Risk-based approach to AI governance

2. **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** - EU (2016)
   - Landmark data privacy law
   - Individual rights over data

3. **AI Bill of Rights (H.R. 6216)** - US Congress (2022)
   - Proposed US AI governance framework
   - Rights-based approach

4. **Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights** - White House (2022)
   - US policy framework for AI
   - Five core principles

5. **UK Algorithms in Decision-Making Report** - UK Parliament
   - Government use of algorithms
   - Accountability and transparency

6. **UK Data Ethics Framework** - UK Government (2021)
   - Ethical data use in public sector
   - Transparency and fairness

7. **Universal Declaration of Human Rights** - UN (1948)
   - Foundational human rights document
   - Digital rights foundation

8. **OECD AI Principles** - OECD (2019)
   - International AI governance framework
   - Human-centric AI

9. **UNESCO Recommendation on Ethics of AI** - UNESCO (2021)
   - Global ethical framework
   - Cultural diversity in AI

## Why These Papers Matter

Legal frameworks define the boundaries of digital sovereignty:
- **GDPR** established individual data rights
- **EU AI Act** sets regulatory precedent globally
- **AI Bill of Rights** frames rights-based approach
- **International frameworks** enable global coordination

Essential for:
- Building compliant systems
- Understanding regulatory landscape
- Advocating for digital rights
- Global coordination
