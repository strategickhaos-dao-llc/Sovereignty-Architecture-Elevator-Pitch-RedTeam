# Category 02: Cryptography & Zero Trust

This category contains 10 papers on cryptographic protocols, zero-knowledge proofs, and secure communication.

## Papers Included

1. **Signal Protocol: Double Ratchet Algorithm**
   - End-to-end encryption for messaging
   - Perfect forward secrecy

2. **Sonic: Zero-Knowledge SNARKs**
   - Succinct non-interactive zero-knowledge proofs
   - Scalable verification

3. **AES Standard (FIPS 197)**
   - Advanced Encryption Standard
   - Most widely used symmetric encryption

4. **Digital Signature Standard (FIPS 186-4)**
   - NIST standard for digital signatures
   - Foundation of authentication

5. **Zerocash: Decentralized Anonymous Payments**
   - Privacy-preserving cryptocurrency
   - zk-SNARKs in practice

6. **PLONK: Permutations over Lagrange-bases**
   - Universal and updatable zk-SNARKs
   - Efficient proof systems

7. **Bulletproofs: Short Proofs for Confidential Transactions**
   - Non-interactive zero-knowledge proofs
   - No trusted setup required

8. **Ed25519: High-speed High-security Signatures**
   - Elliptic curve signatures
   - Fast and secure

9. **Salsa20 and ChaCha20**
   - Stream ciphers for high-speed encryption
   - Modern alternative to AES

## Why These Papers Matter

Privacy and security are fundamental to digital sovereignty:
- **Zero-knowledge proofs** enable privacy without trust
- **Signal Protocol** protects communication
- **Modern cryptography** resists quantum attacks
- **No trusted setup** means no backdoors

Essential for:
- Building secure systems
- Private communication
- Decentralized identity
- Sovereign infrastructure

## Recommended Reading Order

1. **AES** and **Digital Signature Standard** for foundations
2. **Ed25519** for modern signature schemes
3. **Signal Protocol** for practical secure messaging
4. **Zerocash** and **Bulletproofs** for zero-knowledge
5. **PLONK** and **Sonic** for cutting-edge ZK
