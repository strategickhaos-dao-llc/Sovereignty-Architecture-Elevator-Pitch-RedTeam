## Non-Provisional Decision (12 Months Later)

In November 2026, you'll need to decide:

### Option A: File Non-Provisional Patent Application
**If you want full patent protection:**
- Costs: $400-$800 (micro-entity fees) + optional attorney ($5k-$15k)
- Requires: Formal claims, possibly more detailed drawings
- Outcome: Patent examination begins, potential issuance in 1-3 years
- Rights: Exclusive rights to invention for 20 years

### Option B: Let Provisional Expire
**If you only wanted defensive prior art:**
- Cost: $0 (no action required)
- Outcome: Application auto-abandons, but prior art date preserved
- Rights: No patent rights, but competitors can't patent either

### Option C: File as Defensive Publication
**If you want public documentation:**
- Cost: Free (publish the spec yourself) or $130 (USPTO statutory invention registration)
- Outcome: Permanent public record preventing others from patenting
- Rights: No exclusivity, but community can freely implement

**Recommendation**: Evaluate in 10-11 months based on:
- Traction (GitHub stars, Discord community size)
- Competitive landscape (Did Big Tech try to clone it?)

- Philosophy (Do you want monopoly rights or just defensive protection?)
