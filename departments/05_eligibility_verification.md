# Eligibility Verification Department

## Purpose
Conducts detailed verification of Navy Federal eligibility through comprehensive screening of military, DOD, and family connections.

## Key Questions Handled

### Primary Screening Questions

#### 1. Military Service Connection
**Questions to Ask:**
- Are you currently serving in the U.S. military?
  - Active Duty (Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, Space Force, Coast Guard)
  - National Guard
  - Reserves
- Are you a military veteran?
- What was your discharge status?

**Verification Required:**
- DD-214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge)
- Military ID card
- Veterans Affairs documentation

#### 2. Department of Defense Employment
**Questions to Ask:**
- Do you work for the Department of Defense?
- Are you a civilian DOD employee?
- What is your employment status? (GS level, contractor, etc.)

**Verification Required:**
- DOD civilian employee ID
- Employment verification letter
- Pay stub showing DOD employer

#### 3. DOD Contractor Status
**Questions to Ask:**
- Do you work for a DOD contractor?
- What company do you work for?
- What type of DOD contract work do you perform?
- Do you have active security clearance?

**Verification Required:**
- Contractor company verification
- Badge/ID showing contractor status
- Contract documentation
- Security clearance documentation (if applicable)

#### 4. Family Member Eligibility
**Questions to Ask:**
- Do you have immediate family members in Navy Federal?
  - Parents
  - Siblings
  - Spouse
  - Children
- Did any family members serve in the military?
  - Parents
  - Grandparents
  - Siblings
- Are they current Navy Federal members?

**Verification Required:**
- Family member's Navy Federal account number
- Relationship documentation (birth certificate, marriage certificate)
- Family member military service records

#### 5. Household Member Eligibility
**Questions to Ask:**
- Does anyone in your household have Navy Federal membership?
- Do you share the same address?

**Verification Required:**
- Household member's Navy Federal account information
- Proof of shared residence (lease, utility bills)

## Verification Workflow

### Step 1: Initial Screening
```yaml
Process:
  1. Ask primary screening questions
  2. Document all potential eligibility paths
  3. Identify strongest eligibility basis
  4. Determine documentation needed
```

### Step 2: Documentation Collection
```yaml
Process:
  1. Request specific verification documents
  2. Review document authenticity
  3. Verify information matches Navy Federal criteria
  4. Confirm eligibility status
```

### Step 3: Eligibility Confirmation
```yaml
If Eligible:
  1. Document eligibility basis
  2. Proceed to membership application
  3. Route to Business Account Services Department
  
If Not Eligible:
  1. Document reasons
  2. Explore alternative eligibility paths
  3. If no alternatives, route to Alternative Banking Solutions
```

## Special Cases

### Case 1: Indirect Military Connection
**Scenario:** Grandparent served, but not a Navy Federal member
**Decision:** NOT ELIGIBLE - Must be immediate family member who is CURRENT Navy Federal member

### Case 2: Former DOD Contractor
**Scenario:** Previously worked on DOD contracts, now in private sector
**Decision:** NOT ELIGIBLE - Must be CURRENT DOD employee or contractor

### Case 3: Extended Family Navy Federal Member
**Scenario:** Cousin is a Navy Federal member
**Decision:** NOT ELIGIBLE - Must be IMMEDIATE family (parent, sibling, spouse, child)

### Case 4: Roommate Navy Federal Member
**Scenario:** Shares household with Navy Federal member (non-family)
**Decision:** POTENTIALLY ELIGIBLE - Verify shared residence and household status

## Edge Cases & FAQs

### Q: I'm a government contractor, but not DOD-specific
**A:** NOT ELIGIBLE - Must be specifically Department of Defense contractor

### Q: My parent served 30 years ago but never joined Navy Federal
**A:** NOT ELIGIBLE - Parent must be a CURRENT Navy Federal member

### Q: I work for a company that has DOD contracts, but I don't work on them
**A:** NOT ELIGIBLE - Must directly work on DOD contracts

### Q: My spouse is about to join the military
**A:** NOT ELIGIBLE YET - Must wait until spouse is actively serving and you can join as dependent
