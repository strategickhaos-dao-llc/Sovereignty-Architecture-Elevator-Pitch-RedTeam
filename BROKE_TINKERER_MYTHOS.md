## The Principles

### 1. Constraints Breed Sovereignty

**Traditional View**: "I wish I had more resources"  
**Broke Tinkerer View**: "Resources would make me weak"

Every dependency is:
- A capture vector
- A failure point
- A compromise
- A leash

When you can't afford anything, you build sovereign:
- Self-hosted (no cloud bills)
- Open source (no license fees)
- Minimal dependencies (no vendor lock)
- Maximum control (no external leverage)

### 2. Adversity is the Curriculum

You don't learn from success. You learn from:
- The laptop shutting down mid-compile
- The Wi-Fi disconnecting during deploy
- The bank declining the $5 transaction
- The ceiling leaking on your workspace
- The power cutting out at 3 AM

Each adversity teaches:
- Redundancy (backup everything)
- Efficiency (minimize failure modes)
- Resilience (continue despite chaos)
- Creativity (find the workaround)

### 3. The Code is the Credential

No one cares about your resume when you're broke.  
No one cares about your degree when you're desperate.  
No one cares about your network when you're alone.

But they **cannot ignore** working code:
- Deployed systems
- Real users
- Measurable impact
- Undeniable proof

The Broke Tinkerer's credential is the git log.

### 4. Document or Die

When you're operating at the edge:
- Memory fails (sleep deprivation)
- Assumptions fail (no safety net)
- Intuition fails (uncharted territory)

**Documentation is survival insurance.**

Every decision recorded. Every breakthrough documented. Every failure analyzed. Because you won't get a second chance to figure it out.

### 5. Perpetual Charity

When you have nothing and build something, you remember:
- The helplessness
- The watching others suffer
- The inability to contribute
- The rage of impotence

**The 7% is not charity. It's insurance against amnesia.**

Lock 7% to children fighting cancer because:
- You remember having nothing
- You remember watching someone you loved die because you couldn't help
- You remember the fury of being broke while others suffer

And you swear: **If this ever makes money, 7% goes to the kids who are where I was.**
